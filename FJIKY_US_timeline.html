Acceleration for AI data centers: standards, capacity, and products

2026-03-11 → 2026-03-19
In March 2026, Fujikura (via AFL) joined leading optical peers to form the SDM4 MCF MSA to define a four‑core multicore fiber standard for data centers, with intended support from hyperscalers. This move aimed to align products with next‑generation interconnect density needs driven by AI scale-out. Concurrently, the Board adopted a policy to expand optical fiber and SWR/WTC capacity up to threefold across Japan and the U.S., with up to ¥300bn (~$1.9bn) in staged investment linked to the U.S.–Japan AI infrastructure framework. Fujikura also announced a new 4000‑core SWR/WTC cable for domestic data centers, reflecting rapid product innovation toward higher fiber densities.

Foundations and U.S.–Japan AI infra positioning
