Scaling internal RAG and AI infrastructure exploration

2025-03-07 → 2025-12-31
Sumitomo Electric built a groupwide large-capacity RAG foundation in roughly two weeks, enabling about 290,000 employees to conversationally retrieve internal knowledge across hundreds of terabytes and 1.8 million documents. The initiative follows the group’s October 2023 rollout of a safe ChatGPT environment and indicates material internal adoption of OpenAI-based workflows. Management commentary later in the year indicated that customer capex tied to generative AI had increased versus November 2024 levels, with the “Stargate” megaproject cited as a positive factor, implying continued demand for Sumitomo Electric’s data-center optics. In parallel, the company demonstrated dynamic GPU allocation across servers via optical circuit switching—an R&D step aligned with AI accelerator infrastructure needs.

Foundational GenAI product linkage (QuickSolution–ChatGPT)

2024-04-11 → 2024-12-31
Sumitomo Electric Information Systems (a Sumitomo Electric group company) began commercializing a ChatGPT-linked option for its enterprise search product QuickSolution. The option implements retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) to ground ChatGPT responses on enterprise content respecting internal access controls. It supports both OpenAI’s ChatGPT and Microsoft’s Azure OpenAI Service, signaling the group’s first documented product-level linkage to a frontier LLM provider. This introduced a concrete integration surface between the Sumitomo Electric Group’s software stack and OpenAI models, positioning the firm to monetize GenAI use-cases in enterprise search and Q&A while retaining governance over data access.
