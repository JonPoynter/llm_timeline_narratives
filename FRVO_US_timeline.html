Scale-up, IPO, and multi‑GW Google framework (2026)

2026-04-09 → 2026-06-22
In 2026 Fervo accelerated execution ahead of and after its IPO. A five‑year tubular supply agreement with Vallourec (up to $800M potential revenue for the supplier) aimed to support scaled domestic geothermal deployment amid data center hypergrowth. Fervo’s IPO targeted a ~$6.5B valuation with deal size up to ~$1.3B, and shares subsequently debuted on Nasdaq with valuation topping $10B. Q1’26 results marked an inflection: 658 MW of contracted PPAs and a Geothermal Framework Agreement with Google covering up to 3 GW through 2033, with management clarifying the framework is incremental to the 658 MW. Management reiterated PPA pricing ranges (~$100–$130/MWh), highlighted behind‑the‑meter (BTM) pathways to pull forward projects as “speed to power” shapes procurement, and discussed the EGS‑Twin platform integrating physics models with AI (e.g., NVIDIA Omniverse) to improve forecasting. Financing actions included $421.4M non‑recourse project debt, a ~$1.2B capex outlook across the next four quarters, Q1 capex of $172.8M, and ~$2.2B gross proceeds from the May IPO.

NV Energy Clean Transition Tariff and hyperscaler pull (2024–2025)

2024-06-11 → 2025-07-01
Google, NV Energy, and Fervo advanced an innovative procurement structure in Nevada — the Clean Transition Tariff (CTT) — to scale 24/7 clean capacity. Google highlighted inclusion of 115 MW of new enhanced geothermal from Fervo, designed so incremental costs are not borne by other NV Energy customers. Regulatory filings followed, with NV Energy seeking approval to buy power from Fervo’s 115‑MW Corsac Station and sell to Google under the proposed tariff. In May 2025, the Nevada PUC approved the CTT, and reporting indicated the Fervo–Google effort spurred outreach from dozens of hyperscalers and AI companies. Fervo additionally published a 2025 whitepaper positioning an “Enhanced Geothermal Data Center Corridor,” citing OpenAI and Meta activity in Utah, reinforcing AI-driven power demand tailwinds.

2021-05-18 → 2023-11-28
