AI Data Center Acceleration, Standards, Supply Chain, and Financing

2026-01-01 → 2026-06-27
In early 2026, MACOM co-founded and co-chaired the ACC-MSA to define active copper cable specifications aimed at lowering power, cost, and latency—complementing its optical leadership. MACOM also released 448 Gbps per-lane drivers targeting 1.6T/3.2T modules and AOCs. Earnings on May 7, 2026 raised FY26 Data Center growth outlook to >60%, guided strong Q3 sequential growth, cited record-high book-to-bill with robust backlog, and emphasized diversified hyperscaler exposure. Management highlighted a multi-year data center trend with 1.6T leadership, while noting copper opportunities remain TBD and CW lasers likely do not contribute revenue in FY26–FY27. The company advanced capacity within its existing footprint (e.g., ~30% NC wafer capacity increase by YE26), entered a GBP 45M equity/convertible and LTSA transaction with IQE to backstop InP/SiC epi supply, and repaid ~$161M of 2026 converts. Risks cited included AI/DC demand concentration, epi/materials supply chain, and fab yield/scale-up execution. The 10-Q detailed Q2 market mix and top-customer concentration metrics, and management commentary reiterated strong 1.6T deployments and growing coherent interest for power-efficient bandwidth.
