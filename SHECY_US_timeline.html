AI-driven materials capacity build-out and back-end packaging innovation

2024-04-09 → 2024-12-11
Shin-Etsu Chemical initiated a major capacity expansion to serve advanced semiconductor lithography, deciding to build a new production and R&D base in Isesaki, Japan, with a first-phase investment of about ¥83 billion to be completed by 2026. This move positioned the firm to support leading-edge lithography materials, including EUV resists and photomask blanks, amid growing AI-related semiconductor demand. Concurrently, Shin-Etsu disclosed a novel manufacturing approach for semiconductor package substrates using excimer-laser-enabled dual damascene processing to remove the interposer and even bypass traditional photoresist steps. The company previewed these innovations publicly at SEMICON Japan, outlining how integrated materials and equipment advances can shrink features and reduce costs for next-generation AI chip packaging.
