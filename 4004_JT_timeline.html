Foundations: AI partnerships, internal GenAI launch, and Silicon Valley packaging push

2023-09-22 → 2024-07-31
Resonac began formalizing its AI strategy with an external materials informatics tie-up and an internal generative AI initiative. In September 2023, the firm partnered with Matmerize to apply AI-driven polymer informatics to next-generation materials, aiming at 6G and semiconductor use cases. In January 2024, Resonac launched "Chat Resonac," an in‑house generative AI system to unlock institutional knowledge across legacy Showa Denko and Hitachi Chemical datasets while mitigating leakage risk through on‑premise data handling. By February 2024, management articulated a Silicon Valley-centered packaging roadmap aligned to data center and generative AI demand, including a Packaging Solution Center with trial operation in 2024 and full-scale operation in 2025. The firm subsequently expanded AI chip materials capacity (NCF, TIM) and invested for growth. In July 2024, Resonac established the US‑JOINT consortium in Silicon Valley to co‑create next‑generation packaging with leading ecosystem participants, and also disclosed that Chat Resonac runs in a dedicated ChatGPT environment for safe internal use.

AI integration into R&D and manufacturing; ecosystem scaling

2024-11-14 → 2025-12-15
Resonac progressed from foundational AI platforms to concrete R&D acceleration and manufacturing quality gains. It deployed an AI-powered materials exploration capability after a proof-of-concept polymer discovery, and later applied AI to develop a new high-resolution photosensitive film for AI processors. The firm also introduced deep-learning-based inspection with material-specific training data, cutting false detection rates in spherical alumina inspection and improving accuracy by around 40%. Concurrently, Resonac expanded its collaborative packaging ecosystem with the 27-member JOINT3 consortium, leveraging learnings from JOINT/JOINT2 and US-JOINT to advance 2.5D/3D integration, HBM, and chiplets with partners such as Lam Research.

US-JOINT R&D base activation and AI-led 26Q1 momentum

2026-04-20 → 2026-05-13
Resonac activated the US-JOINT R&D center in Silicon Valley, the first U.S. base focused on advanced packaging technology co-development, to validate concepts with major advanced semiconductor users and accelerate commercialization.
