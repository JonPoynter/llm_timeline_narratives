Building AI-adjacent optics and U.S. onshore manufacturing options

2025-11-13 → 2025-12-04
In late 2025, UMC advanced its role in AI-adjacent optics and onshore manufacturing. The Metalenz partnership brought Polar ID face authentication to mass production using UMC’s 40nm process and wafer-on-wafer bonding, signaling participation in machine vision and AI use cases. Initial AI positioning and U.S. process collaboration

2023-07-26 → 2024-01-25
UMC publicly framed its AI stance in mid-2023, noting near-term demand uncertainty while emphasizing preparations to meet AI-related customer needs. The company maintained capital spending plans despite cyclical softness, signaling intent to support AI-led demand when it materializes. In early 2024, UMC announced a strategic 12nm process collaboration with Intel, adding a U.S.-manufactured FinFET-capable platform targeted to begin production in 2027. This expands geographic optionality for customers and aligns UMC with high-growth end markets including communications and networking that intersect with AI workloads at the edge and in infrastructure.
