Open chiplet standards and ecosystem groundwork

2022-03-02 → 2022-10-18
ASE began laying AI-adjacent groundwork by joining with leading semiconductor, foundry, IP, and cloud companies to found the Universal Chiplet Interconnect Express (UCIe) consortium in March 2022. The initiative targets an interoperable, multi‑vendor chiplet ecosystem, which could lower development time and cost for advanced packaging customers and broaden addressability across hyperscaler and compute vendors. By August 2022 the UCIe Consortium had been formally incorporated with ASE as a founding board member alongside peers and major cloud and platform companies; additional members such as Alibaba and NVIDIA joined the board. In October 2022, ASE presented advanced packaging and chiplet design kit topics at the Open Compute Project (OCP) Global Summit, signaling alignment with open hardware communities relevant to large‑scale AI systems.

2026 AI packaging ramp, capex intensity, and execution risks

2026-02-05 → 2026-04-29
In February 2026, ASE indicated its advanced packaging business could double to roughly $3.2B in 2026, with incremental machinery capex of about $1.5B on top of prior year levels; Reuters also noted SPIL’s role as a packaging supplier for NVIDIA AI chips. By late April, ASE raised its LEAP (advanced packaging) 2026 revenue target to over $3.5B, reflecting stronger visibility but also higher execution demands. Q1 2026 capex reached $1.0B, and management flagged that elevated capex likely persists into 2027 with any 2026 funding gap primarily financed via additional borrowing. Operationally, wafer sort was identified as a near‑term bottleneck; two buildings were acquired for LEAP with progressive ramp starting early 2027 after readiness and tool move‑in. The CFO cited CPO collaboration with foundry and end customers while describing CoWoS as "further away,” and cautioned LEAP profitability had not yet reached “blue sky,” even if theoretically accretive. Management also expressed confidence that AI‑adjacent peripheral content and other end markets could sustain ~13% growth ex‑LEAP in 2026, and reported mix shift toward advanced packaging at 49% of ATM.
