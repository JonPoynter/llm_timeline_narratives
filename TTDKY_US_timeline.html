FY2026–FY2027 AI infrastructure and storage ramp

2026-04-28 → 2026-06-30
At FY2026 results (Apr 28, 2026), TDK emphasized elevated capital intensity and product focus aligned to the AI ecosystem—particularly AI data centers and nearline HDD. FY2026 capex was ¥298.6bn, with a plan for higher capex and R&D in FY2027, and an explicit FY27 capex plan of ¥370bn prioritizing small rechargeable batteries, HAMR‑related head equipment, and suspension capacity. Management highlighted AI‑linked demand lanes across passives (electrolytics, inductors, MLCC), HDD heads/suspensions, medium‑capacity batteries (BBUs), and semi‑cap equipment (load ports, flip‑chip bonders), while pushing to expand AI use‑cases in passives. Operationally, TDK flagged robust data‑center HDD demand, upgraded head volume growth to ~40% YoY with suspensions up ~20% for the year, targeted about 20% YoY profit growth in Magnetic Application Products despite higher depreciation, and cited an external assumption of AI server units +21% YoY to frame demand. The company also noted risk that a reversal in nearline HDD build plans or AI‑driven storage mix shifts could compress margins.

AI-enabled consumer device integrations (Meta, Google)

2023-10-13 → 2024-10-31
TDK’s InvenSense sensor portfolio appears in flagship AI-enabled consumer devices. iFixit's Meta Quest 3 controller teardown in October 2023 identified a TDK‑InvenSense 6‑axis IMU on the controller board, evidencing design‑win penetration at Meta. In 2024, TechInsights teardowns of Google's Pixel 8 and Pixel 8a point to TDK‑InvenSense barometric and motion sensors on the main boards, indicating continued attach across Google's Tensor‑based smartphones. These integrations connect TDK to two frontier AI ecosystems—Meta (Llama) and Google (Gemini/DeepMind)—via hardware used to deliver AI features on‑device and in companion services. While the sources are teardowns (not commercial terms), they validate ongoing product integration exposure to leading AI providers’ device platforms.
