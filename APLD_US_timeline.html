Multi‑hyperscaler expansion, financing, and execution risk management

2026-01-07 → 2026-06-10
On January 7, 2026, Applied Digital disclosed it had signed leases with two hyperscalers across two North Dakota campuses: CoreWeave at 400MW (~$11B prospective lease revenue) and a U.S. investment‑grade hyperscaler at 200MW (~$5B), totaling ~$16B prospective lease revenue. In April and May 2026, the company added a 300MW, ~$7.5B lease at Delta Forge 1 and a further 300MW, ~$7.5B lease at Polaris Forge 3 with the same investment‑grade hyperscaler, taking total contracted lease revenue above $23B and then to $31B across four campuses (1,200MW net critical IT load). The April 8, 2026 fiscal Q3 release detailed a ~$15.52B minimum contracted payments schedule, construction and energization milestones (first 100MW liquid‑cooled HPC online; +350MW under construction), and a major secured notes issuance ($2.15B, 6.750% due 2031). It also addressed credit enhancements (CoreWeave SPV guarantees and a $50M LC), leverage and refinancing goals (~5–6x NOI, ABS markets), grid/power strategy (ND through 2028; Base Electron thereafter), and operational guidance (PF1 RFS July 1 with revenue step‑ups in August/November). Risks were explicitly flagged: South Dakota campus pause after a tax exemption failed, customer concentration (A 56%, B 30%, C 14%), high capex and ~$2.7B debt load, execution timing dependencies, Cloud Services reclassification/ChronoScale combination with a $59.7M remeasurement loss, and negative free cash flow approximately $(1.62)B for 9M FY2026. On June 10, 2026, market coverage cited a rally in APLD shares following a 15‑year hyperscaler lease, referencing approximately $5.2B revenue over the term, reflecting investor focus on the expanding long‑term backlog.
