AI productization and 26Q1 acceleration

2026-04-15 → 2026-04-29
Equinix announced Fabric Intelligence, an AI‑native suite enabling natural‑language and agentic workflows for network operations, with MCP servers integrating with leading AI clients like Claude Code and OpenAI Codex. The product entered preview with demos at Google Cloud Next 2026. Equinix also published a joint perspective with Palo Alto Networks on securing enterprise AI across multiple model providers using private, low‑latency connectivity. In 26Q1, AI‑linked indicators accelerated: ~60% of largest deals were AI‑related, Fabric bookings rose ~70% y/y, and annualized gross bookings reached $378M with record cabinet backlog and strong presales. Interconnection growth outpaced stabilized assets, liquid‑cooling deployments scaled to 36 active with ~50% q/q growth, and Fabric revenue grew 26% y/y. Management reiterated power availability as the main constraint, noted >90% of 2026 energy costs were hedged, and highlighted a 3 GW land pipeline, while a Hampton xScale lease economics recognition shifted to Q2. Equinix also disclosed that eight of the top 10 AI model providers and four of the top five neo clouds were expanding on its platform with over 110 network nodes.
