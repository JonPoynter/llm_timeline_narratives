26Q1 acceleration, funding inflows, power buildout and benchmarks

2026-05-13 → 2026-06-16
In 1Q26, Nebius reported AI cloud revenue of $389.7M (≈98% of group), ARR of $1.9B (+~54% q/q), and a 45% adjusted EBITDA margin. Operating cash flow was $2.26B, bolstered by record customer prepayments, while capex purchases totaled ~$2.47B, yielding implied negative free cash flow of ≈$(214.9)M. Deferred revenue rose by ~$3.20B sequentially, and the company ended the quarter with $9.3B cash and >90% of its initial 2026 capex range secured by cash and commitments. Nebius raised its 2026 capex guide to $20–$25B to support 2027 capacity, expanded contracted power to >3.5 GW, and announced a new 1.2 GW Pennsylvania campus (with a ramp to 1.2 GW by ~2030). Deliveries to Microsoft and Meta remained on schedule, with the first Meta contract delivered in Q1. Management detailed a margin cadence with Q2 lower, then rising in Q3/Q4, and reiterated FY26 ARR and margin targets. Risks cited included capital intensity and financing execution, potential power and permitting delays, earnings volatility from non-cash revaluations, and customer concentration. Externally, Nebius was named an NVIDIA Exemplar Cloud on GB300 and posted strong MLPerf Training 6.0 results on both HGX B300 and GB300 NVL72. News flow also pointed to onsite power initiatives, with CNBC reporting Nebius deployments of Bloom fuel cells at data centers — indicative of ongoing efforts to secure resilient power for AI workloads.
