Licensing Build‑out, Newsroom Tools, and Legal Assertiveness

2024-10-21 → 2025-10-08
Following the OpenAI accord, News Corp pursued a dual‑track strategy: scaling AI‑centric products and asserting its IP rights. Dow Jones built an AI marketplace in Factiva, expanding to nearly 5,000 publishers, and launched Smart Summary for generative summaries with traceability and attribution. News Corp Australia deployed an internal, enterprise-grade tool, NewsGPT, integrating multiple frontier models for secure newsroom use. In parallel, the company litigated against alleged unauthorized scraping and sought clarity on rights. Dow Jones and New York Post sued Perplexity; Brave sought declaratory relief that its summaries and tools did not infringe. Governance disclosures highlighted implementation of the OpenAI deal and ongoing negotiations with Google, Amazon, and Meta, alongside active litigation with Perplexity and Brave.
