Silicon photonics acceleration and AI market framing

2025-03-19 → 2025-07-21
In early 2025, Soitec highlighted the acceleration of silicon photonics for AI datacenters, citing the industry’s move toward co‑packaged optics (CPO) and joining the SEMI Silicon Photonics Industry Alliance. The company pointed to NVIDIA’s unveiling of its first CPO products as a demand signal for optical interconnects. Soitec’s Universal Registration Document quantified the broader market tailwinds, noting around US$400 billion cumulative AI infrastructure capex by hyperscalers through 2025 and a forecast for optical transmission markets to quadruple by 2030. In July, Soitec clarified its commercial model: it does not sell directly to Nvidia, but benefits indirectly via foundry customers that manufacture chips and optical components for AI workloads.

Foundation agreements and substrate partnerships

2017-09-19 → 2024-06-13
Soitec’s AI-relevant positioning is underpinned by long-standing substrate supply relationships and packaging innovations with major foundry partners. In 2017, the company entered a five-year FD‑SOI wafer supply agreement with GLOBALFOUNDRIES, reinforcing industrial scale and security of supply for FD‑SOI technologies. In June 2024, Soitec extended its partnership with UMC to underpin the industry’s first 3D IC solution for RF‑SOI, enabling die stacking with wafer-to-wafer bonding and significant area reduction. While not explicitly AI workload–specific, these agreements strengthened Soitec’s role in the semiconductor stack that ultimately services AI and high‑bandwidth communications demand.
