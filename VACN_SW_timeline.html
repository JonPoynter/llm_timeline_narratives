Foundations and AI demand framing

2021-03-03 → 2024-03-04
VAT Group articulated early that artificial intelligence, IoT, and cloud computing were secular demand drivers for semiconductors, and by extension for vacuum valve equipment. In its 2020 Annual Report (published March 2021), the company highlighted expectations for the semiconductor market to exceed USD 640 billion within five years and approach USD 1 trillion by decade-end, alongside at-least-6% CAGR in wafer fab equipment (WFE) investments. By March 2024, the 2023 Annual Report explicitly tied the mass adoption of AI—referencing OpenAI and Bing—to the need for leading-edge chips, emphasizing VAT’s technology and market leadership in advanced semiconductor manufacturing and its positioning to capture the resulting growth.

AI megacycle recognized; 2025 record WFE and 2026 ramp with strong order metrics

2026-03-02 → 2026-04-16
VAT’s 2025 reporting period captured a surge in AI-related infrastructure investment. Group results and the Annual Report emphasized hyperscaler capex of over USD 400 billion in 2025 (up from USD 230 billion in 2024), and signaled 2026 AI data center capex around USD 740 billion. The 2025 media release noted record front-end WFE at USD 115 billion and reaffirmed VAT’s 71% market share in semiconductor and related vacuum valves. The Q1 2026 media release and trading update projected 2026 WFE at roughly USD 130 billion and industry AI investments above USD 750 billion, while direct China business remained on a growth trajectory. VAT’s Q1 2026 filings detailed a book‑to‑bill of ~1.6x and a CHF 431.3m backlog, with guidance for Q2 sales of CHF 265–295m, execution of a 20–30% QoQ output ramp, and an H2 run‑rate plan of at least CHF 400m per quarter. Management reiterated ambitions to outgrow WFE (assumed at $130–135bn) up to ~2x and flagged 2027 as a potential record year supported by over 110 fabs under construction and AI‑driven capacity additions in logic and DRAM. Risks included timing sensitivity to AI‑led fab investments and concentration in leading-edge logic and HBM/DRAM, plus conflict-related logistics issues that shifted CHF 20–25m of shipments out of Q1. Mitigations such as dual‑sourcing critical components were highlighted to improve supply resilience.
