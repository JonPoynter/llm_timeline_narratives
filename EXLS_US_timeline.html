Agentic AI expansion, Google Cloud strategic services, NVIDIA integrations, and 26Q1 financials

2026-03-11 → 2026-06-30
In early 2026, EXL broadened its product stack with agentic AI and formalized a strategic services collaboration with Google Cloud. The company launched EXL Agent Studio and a Governance Hub housing more than 40 specialized models. Google named EXL a strategic services collaborator to accelerate AI-led transformation across priority industries. Financially, 26Q1 showed pronounced AI mix: Data & AI-led revenue reached $341.6M (~60% of total) with NRR above 1. Management raised FY2026 guidance despite FX headwinds, flagged reinvestment in AI R&D, and outlined a shift toward fixed-fee and outcome-based models. Capital structure updates included modest operating cash flow, planned FY26 capex of ~$50–55M, and net debt of ~$151M. EXL also integrated NVIDIA’s Transaction Foundation Model developer workflow for financial institutions, and announced a definitive agreement to acquire iMerit for up to $310M to deepen foundation model training, evaluation and RL capabilities. A case study highlighted tangible client savings from an agentic AI IVR built on Google’s Gemini platform.

Foundational hyperscaler alignments and vertical AI builds

2023-11-15 → 2024-09-26
EXL began formalizing AI go-to-market routes through hyperscalers. In November 2023, EXL was recognized as a Premier Level Partner for Google Cloud in the Service Engagement Model, validating its Google Cloud delivery proficiency. In February 2024, EXL simultaneously expanded with Microsoft and AWS: it announced a collaboration using Microsoft Azure OpenAI Service to co‑develop generative AI solutions, and rolled out cloud‑native generative AI solutions on AWS built using Amazon Bedrock via EXL’s AI workbench. By mid‑2024 EXL leaned into NVIDIA’s full‑stack AI platform to develop production‑grade AI across insurance, healthcare, banking and other sectors, and then launched a specialized Insurance LLM leveraging NVIDIA AI Enterprise. The Insurance LLM emphasized domain specialization and reported internal accuracy improvements versus generic frontier models, positioning EXL for industry‑specific AI work.
