Scaling data-center AI business, investments, and provider choices

2026-01-27 → 2026-06-27
In early 2026 Mitsubishi Electric deepened data-center alignment through venture investments and reported strong AI-linked order momentum. The ME Innovation Fund invested in Lucend to optimize data-center operations using Transparent AI across metrics derived from 300+ billion sensor readings. The company also invested in Japan-based Sakana AI to augment its Serendie digital platform with next-generation foundation-model technologies. The FY26 Q4 disclosures highlighted sharp AI-driven improvements: FA orders up materially on smartphone/AI capex; optical-device orders +393% YoY with revenue expected to grow 30%+; FA Systems Q4 orders +130% YoY; and management reiterated a prior Q3 data‑center opportunity sizing of approximately ¥120–130bn. Separately, technical literature documented active use of Anthropic’s Claude Haiku 4.5 on Amazon Bedrock (Tokyo region) with tool-calling and data-handling guarantees, signaling a concrete provider choice and architecture designed for future model portability. The firm also signed a non-binding MoU with Quantinuum to explore quantum-computing applications across industrial domains.
