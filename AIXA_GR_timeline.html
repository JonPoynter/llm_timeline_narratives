AI datacom-driven optoelectronics inflection and capital actions

2026-04-30 → 2026-05-19
In Q1 2026, AIXTRON marked a clear inflection in optoelectronics demand tied to AI data center optical interconnect upgrades. Management highlighted multi-tool orders, visibility beyond 2026, and a mix skewed toward G10 systems. Order intake and backlog expanded materially, supported by advance payments. The results speech detailed a fundamental architectural shift from copper to 800G/1600G optics in AI data centers, with customers ramping EML and PIC-based lasers. Management framed the potential market need at 80–100 G10 tools per year and €300–400m annual G10 sales in certain scenarios. To reinforce flexibility, AIXTRON issued a zero-coupon €450m convertible due 2031 and announced a new Malaysia production site as a strategic footprint move. R&D investment increased, with a 2026 plan of ~€90m. Risks included H2-weighted shipments, InP wafer supply bottlenecks, Malaysia ramp execution, and FX headwinds. Customer momentum was corroborated by Lumentum placing multiple G10-AsP orders to expand high-speed optical solutions for AI networks.

MicroLED and GaN-on-Si groundwork

2016-03-02 → 2022-02-23
AIXTRON's role in advanced display and LED epitaxy was established years before the current AI data center cycle. In 2016 and 2018, Plessey selected AIXTRON's AIX G5+ C MOCVD systems to expand GaN-on-Si LED and microLED capacity, with installations supporting monolithic microLED development. By 2022, additional customers such as HC Semitek were using AIXTRON's AIX 2800G4-TM and AIX G5+ C systems for microLED production. These customer decisions cemented AIXTRON's position across next-generation display supply chains. While not directly tied to LLM providers, these wins expanded AIXTRON's installed base and process leadership in III-V and GaN epitaxy platforms that later benefited from AI-driven optical interconnect demand. The groundwork in microLED and GaN-on-Si also supported diversification across photonics and display verticals.
