AI demand inflection, customer expansions, and raised FY26 outlook

2026-01-01 → 2026-06-27
Through early 2026 Arteris reported broad AI and chiplet-driven demand across data center/HPC/HBM and automotive, alongside multiple customer wins and ecosystem collaborations. Notable adoption highlights included expansions with a leading hyperscaler, an HBM memory supplier, Renesas R-Car Gen5 ADAS, and continued traction across NXP, Black Sesame, and Blaize. Financially, RPO reached $118.3M (+33% y/y) by Q1, ACV+royalties rose to $92.8M (+39% y/y), and variable royalties accelerated, with record April deal flow. Management raised its FY26 outlook for ACV+royalties and provided guidance for Q2. Risks were explicitly noted: royalty ramps depend on customer execution in AI/data center and automotive; management will cease quarterly FCF guidance from Q2; and Arteris published a security-focused blog highlighting accelerating AI-enabled vulnerability discovery by frontier models.

Ecosystem standards and AI data center alignment

2025-01-01 → 2025-12-31
Arteris joined the UALink Consortium to support standards-based scale-up for AI accelerators, aligning its NoC capabilities with a leading open ecosystem initiative formed by hyperscalers and semiconductor leaders. The consortium aims to enable direct load/store/atomic operations between accelerators, targeting breakthrough performance for emerging AI workloads. This ecosystem positioning complements Arteris’s role with AI and HPC customers and enhances optionality for future product interoperability as AI accelerator interconnect standards evolve.

Initial AI hardware wins and LLM-era positioning

2024-01-01 → 2024-12-31
Arteris began 2024 with a notable AI-accelerator design-in win, as Rain AI selected its FlexNoC 5 interconnect IP for a next-generation AI accelerator family. This validated Arteris’s role as a critical enabler in AI silicon, particularly for high-bandwidth, low-latency data movement on chip. In parallel, the company published thought-leadership discussing NoC build-versus-buy tradeoffs and even referenced emerging LLMs (ChatGPT 3.5/4.0) as conceptual aids for development planning. While this did not imply a provider dependency, it highlighted Arteris’s engagement with the broader AI discourse and its implications for semiconductor system IP design.
