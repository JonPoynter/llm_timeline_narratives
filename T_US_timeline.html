Enterprise GenAI launch and scale-up

2023-05-18 → 2024-12-31
In mid‑2023 AT&T began using Azure OpenAI Service and launched its internal GenAI platform, Ask AT&T, initially built on OpenAI’s ChatGPT functionality within an AT&T‑dedicated Azure tenant. Axios reported early productivity gains of 25%–50% for coding tasks, while Microsoft described multiple automation and employee-assist use cases. Ask AT&T rapidly scaled: by September 2023 over 30,000 employees had access, with the platform designed to be interoperable across models including Meta’s LLaMA 2 and open-source Falcon, and with close collaboration with NVIDIA. By December 2023 usage exceeded 68,000 employees. Through 2024, AT&T deepened cloud connectivity (Google Gold Level Verified Peering Provider) and reported roughly 1 billion tokens per day across diverse workflows, signaling broad internal adoption and cost/accuracy focus.

Pre-genAI cloud-network alignment and edge partnerships

2021-06-30 → 2023-05-31
AT&T laid foundational cloud alliances ahead of its generative AI push. In June 2021, AT&T announced it would run its mobility network on Microsoft’s Azure for Operators, beginning with the 5G core and transferring carrier-grade Network Cloud technology—an architectural pivot that set up cloud-native operations and access to hyperscale AI and edge tooling. A month later, AT&T and Google Cloud expanded a 5G and edge collaboration, combining AT&T MEC with Google Cloud capabilities including AI/ML and planning deployment across 15+ zones. These infra moves positioned AT&T to integrate AI at both the network and enterprise layers in subsequent years.
