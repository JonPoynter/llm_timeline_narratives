Micron transaction and AI‑centric foundry pivot

2026-03-16 → 2026-04-21
PSMC completed a $1.8 billion transaction with Micron for the Tongluo P5 plant while commencing collaboration at PSMC’s Hsinchu site to provide HBM/PWF foundry services and advance memory process technologies—supporting its 3D AI Foundry ambitions. Shortly after, PSMC’s Q1’26 disclosure formalized a strategic pivot to an AI‑centric foundry model built on three pillars: Memory, Logic, and 3D AI Foundry (interposer/WoW). Management outlined execution milestones: a 12‑inch IPD ramp beginning Q2’26; HBM‑related PWF tool‑in by Q3’26, pilot in Q4’26, mass production around Q4’27 with ~20k wpm at full ramp; and a Micron 1P DRAM node co‑development timeline with equipment move‑in Q1’27, trials in H1’28, and mass production in H2’28. 2026 capex was set at ~$512m. Risks cited included execution on the 3D AI Foundry ramp, capex‑driven FCF pressure, and pricing/cycle sensitivity, while demand tailwinds included multi‑year AI customer DRAM agreements and pricing benefits beginning from June.

3D AI Foundry build-out and ecosystem collaborations

2024-09-05 → 2025-10-17
PSMC accelerated its 3D AI Foundry positioning through technology announcements and partnerships. In September–October 2024 it unveiled Logic‑DRAM multi-layer wafer stacking and certified 2.5D interposers, and disclosed investment plans at its Tongluo plant to launch 3D AI foundry services, with ramp targeted for 2H25 and collaborations including AP Memory and mentions of AMD. In May–June 2025, PSMC showcased a broader AI ecosystem at Computex with partners across IP, design services, memory architectures, and 3D packaging, and announced a collaboration with Soitec to supply TLT‑ready 300mm substrates to demonstrate advanced wafer‑level 3D stacking. In October 2025, PSMC’s chairman participated in a Paris AI and Quantum roundtable, underscoring international engagement, though without immediate commercial disclosures.

Japan JV exploration and withdrawal

2023-07-05 → 2024-09-27
PSMC and SBI Holdings announced a basic agreement in July 2023 to form a preparatory company aimed at establishing a semiconductor factory in Japan, and by October 2023 selected the Second Northern Sendai Central Industrial Park in Miyagi as the planned fab site, signing a memorandum of understanding with local stakeholders. The plan was premised on receiving a government subsidy. By late September 2024, SBI disclosed that, at PSMC’s request, the joint business in Japan would be dissolved, signaling execution or strategic challenges to continuing the Japan fab initiative. This rollback removed a potential geographic expansion lever but did not directly affect PSMC’s AI-foundry pivot elsewhere.
