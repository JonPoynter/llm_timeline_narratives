Pre‑AI groundwork and early hyperscale storage adoption

2019-11-05 → 2020-10-26
Western Digital’s AI-adjacent posture traces back to security and cloud-scale storage building blocks. In 2019, the company joined Google and lowRISC to back OpenTitan, an open-source silicon root of trust effort aimed at transparency and security in data-centric platforms, including machine-learning and IoT contexts. This positioned WD within an ecosystem concerned with trustworthy infrastructure underpinning AI-era systems. In 2020, WD’s host-managed SMR nearline drives continued gaining marquee cloud adoption as Dropbox qualified Ultrastar DC HC650 20TB SMR HDDs, a signal that hyperscale customers were embracing higher-efficiency archival and warm data storage that later became foundational for AI data pipelines.
