Scaling AI fabric and CXL programs; Amazon warrant; Q1’26 Scorpio X progress

2026-02-10 → 2026-05-05
In February 2026, Astera entered a new warrant agreement with Amazon enabling up to 3.26 million shares at $142.82, vesting upon up to $6.5 billion in product purchase tranches across smart fabric switch, signal conditioning, and optical engine products, with an exercise period through 2033. Shortly after, the company’s 10-K highlighted a customer base of major hyperscalers and AI accelerator vendors and described its interoperable ecosystem work via the Interop Lab. In May 2026 Q1 results, management reported initial shipments of the Scorpio X-Series 320-lane AI fabric switch and broader portfolio progress (Scorpio P-Series, Smart Cable Modules, COSMOS software). They highlighted collaboration on UALink 2.0, X shipping now with high-radix ramping in 2H26 and X expected to surpass P by year-end. They reiterated optics roadmaps (NPO 2027, CPO 2028), UALink platform ramps in 2027 with higher value than PCIe, and multiple 2027 revenue programs (NVLink Fusion custom with NVIDIA and a hyperscaler; a second custom CXL design). Management also noted supply secured through YE26, rising ASPs with switch size and features like Hypercast and in-network compute, and several risks: customer concentration, ramp execution, standards/competitive dynamics, and warrant-linked pricing effects.

Foundation: Amazon-linked warrants and open interconnect consortium

2024-03-08 → 2024-12-31
Astera Labs set foundational commercial alignment with Amazon via customer-linked stock warrants tied to substantial purchase tranches across product families, originally detailed in its March 2024 S-1/A. These warrant structures connected vesting to up to $650 million in aggregate payments across Aries, Taurus, and Leo products, alongside an additional 2023 warrant for future development tranches. Concurrently, Astera solidified its role in open accelerator interconnects by joining leadership at the newly incorporated Ultra Accelerator Link (UALink) Consortium in October 2024. This positioned the company with major cloud and silicon players, including Google and Meta, to influence standards for scale-up AI connectivity.
