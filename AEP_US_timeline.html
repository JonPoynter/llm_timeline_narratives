Interim load reallocation amid Intel delay: Meta taps AEP Ohio substation

2025-11-26 → 2025-12-04
With Intel’s chip-fab timeline in flux, AEP Ohio and Meta sought PUCO approval for a three-year arrangement allowing Meta’s nearby data center to draw power from the completed but idle Green Chapel substation originally built for Intel. The agreement would cover the substation’s full cost and temporarily re-allocate capacity. Subsequent reporting indicated the plan contemplates up to 250 MW for Meta (initially 120 MW), with Intel’s 500 MW allotment restored in 2029. This illustrates AEP’s flexibility in staging capacity to serve near-term hyperscaler demand while preserving long-term commitments.

Foundational hyperscaler load and infrastructure (New Albany origins)

2017-11-02 → 2020-07-01
AEP’s data-center engagement in Central Ohio began well before the current AI surge, anchored by New Albany’s mission-critical infrastructure. In 2017, AEP supported Facebook’s (now Meta) large New Albany build with dedicated substation and transmission work, establishing a template for serving very large, power-intensive customers. By 2019–2020, Google commenced significant investment in the region, with AEP’s infrastructure cited as an enabler. AEP Energy then signed a long-term 400 MW renewable PPA to serve Google’s New Albany data center, aligning clean energy supply with hyperscaler load requirements.
