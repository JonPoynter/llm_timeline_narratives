Scale-up in bookings/backlog and risk management

2026-02-05 → 2026-04-23
Across 25Q4 and 26Q1, Digital Realty reported a step-up in AI-oriented demand, with DLR-share backlog rising from $817 million at year-end 2025 to $1.0 billion by 26Q1 and new bookings increasing from $175 million (Q4 at DLR share) to $423 million in 26Q1. Management highlighted the largest hyperscale lease in company history, record 0–1 MW plus interconnection bookings, and pricing power as escalators moved to 3–4% and hyperscale terms around 15 years. The company guided 2026 net development CapEx to $3.5–$4.0 billion with targeted 10%+ stabilized yields, raised ~$1.3 billion via its ATM program, and cited ~3 GW operating with 1.2 GW under construction (~60% pre-leased). Risks include a longer average commencement lag (to ~19 months) driven by a single 200 MW phased lease, execution and power availability, and heightened concentration from very large hyperscale deals. The firm also noted an ~873-acre Atlanta land bank and a 15 MW development in Cyberjaya.
