Ecosystem anchoring and liquid-cooling standards

2024-03-18 → 2025-10-10
Vertiv established early, durable footholds in the AI infrastructure ecosystem through vendor alliances and open-standard contributions. In March 2024, the company joined the NVIDIA Partner Network as a Solution Advisor: Consultant, expanding its advisory and solution-integration role around accelerated computing infrastructure. In October 2024, Vertiv deepened that collaboration by co-developing a complete power and cooling blueprint for the NVIDIA GB200 NVL72 platform. Concurrently, Vertiv advanced open standards that de-risk vendor lock-in for customers and broaden its addressable market. The company co-authored the OCP 30°C coolant roadmap in October 2024 and aligned product efforts with Google’s OCP Project Deschutes CDUs. By October 2025, Vertiv’s CoolChip CDU was showcased as built to Google’s Deschutes specification, highlighting liquid-cooling leadership relevant to AI-scale thermal loads. Vertiv also positioned for longer-term power availability constraints via a July 2025 collaboration with Oklo to co-develop power and thermal management solutions using onsite advanced nuclear energy, underscoring Vertiv’s strategy to pair innovation with resilient energy options for hyperscale and colocation data centers.
