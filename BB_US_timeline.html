QNX expansion into physical AI; NVIDIA ecosystem alignment; backlog and guidance updates

2026-04-01 → 2026-06-30
BlackBerry’s QNX business accelerated into 2026 with a strategic push beyond automotive into robotics/physical AI, increased OEM activity around Alloy Kore, and a growing, diversified backlog. Early access trials for Alloy Kore with Mercedes‑Benz and planned BMW ‘Neue Klasse’ integration signaled momentum. Management highlighted the potential for Alloy Kore to significantly impact the ~$950M QNX royalty backlog (up ~$85M y/y), with GEM contributing ~20% of backlog and ~10% of revenue and exhibiting margin‑accretive pricing dynamics. The company released QNX Hypervisor 8.0 for Safety, announced Leapmotor’s selection of SDP 8.0 and Hypervisor 8.0, and flagged retrofit paths for Alloy Kore that could uplift existing backlog. Q1 FY2027 results showed strong QNX growth, a higher FY2027 revenue outlook, Secure Communications ARR of $220M with 92% DBNRR, and the first cash‑positive fiscal Q1 in years, while also noting delivery/adoption and competitive risks around new offerings and ecosystem collaborations.

Portfolio reshaping and on-device AI posture

2025-01-01 → 2026-03-31
In early 2025, BlackBerry closed the sale of its Cylance business to Arctic Wolf, while retaining a commercial relationship as customer and reseller, and remaining a shareholder—reshaping its direct AI cybersecurity product exposure but preserving strategic optionality. In parallel, the company articulated a conservative, enterprise‑grade posture for mobile AI. In March 2026, BlackBerry published guidance warning that using Google Gemini within BlackBerry Dynamics apps can result in data leakage to Google, directing administrators to apply DLP controls. Shortly thereafter, BlackBerry introduced Work Smart Tools, stating that features process data locally on-device using Apple Foundation Models on iOS or Google Gemini Nano on Android, ensuring email and AI‑generated content never leaves the device.

Early enterprise GenAI positioning and Cylance Assistant launch

2023-08-08 → 2024-12-31
BlackBerry began signaling its enterprise stance on generative AI risk and opportunity in 2023. In August 2023, the company published survey research highlighting that a large share of enterprises were moving to restrict ChatGPT and other GenAI apps on work devices due to data security, privacy, and reputational concerns. In October 2023, BlackBerry announced a new Generative AI powered assistant for SOC teams, emphasizing use of private LLMs for accuracy and data privacy. In April 2024, BlackBerry brought this to general availability as Cylance Assistant, a generative AI cybersecurity advisor delivered via Amazon Bedrock, framed with privacy-preserving principles that do not share customer data to train models. Bedrock’s model choice provides an abstraction layer over multiple LLM providers, potentially improving flexibility while limiting provider lock‑in.
