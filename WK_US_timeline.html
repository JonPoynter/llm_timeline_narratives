Foundation and multi-cloud AI integration

2023-08-01 → 2024-12-31
Workiva formally introduced generative AI across its cloud platform in August 2023, positioning capabilities as deeply integrated into reporting workflows while committing to responsible use and data privacy. The initial rollout emphasized an open ecosystem approach, allowing customers to select among leading large language models, notably those available via Google Cloud and Microsoft Azure. By October 2023, Workiva clarified its architecture: the company does not train its own LLM and instead partners with Google, AWS, and Microsoft through Vertex AI, Bedrock (Anthropic), and Azure OpenAI APIs. This multi-provider design underscored data protection and portability principles. Operationally, Workiva encountered regional model performance issues in late 2024. The company temporarily reverted EU customers from Claude 3.5 Sonnet back to Claude 3 Sonnet, highlighting pragmatic risk management and the value of maintaining multiple providers.
