Initial AI-driven power campus deals in Pennsylvania

2025-04-02 → 2025-11-20
EQT’s AI-linked demand exposure surfaced in 2025 via two marquee natural-gas power projects designed to serve hyperscale data centers: the Homer City Energy Campus and the Shippingport Power Station redevelopment. On April 2, 2025, owners of the retired Homer City coal plant announced a $10 billion plan to convert the site into a 4.5 GW gas-powered data center campus. By July 15, 2025, Homer City Redevelopment disclosed an agreement in principle naming EQT as exclusive gas supplier for a 4.4 GW facility with up to 665,000 MMBtus/day of fuel, while on the same day The Frontier Group’s Shippingport project indicated EQT intended to be the plant’s gas supplier for an estimated 800 MMcf/d of demand. EQT corroborated progress on July 23, 2025, stating it was working to finalize 20-year definitive gas-supply agreements for both Homer City and Shippingport, with phased ramps beginning in 2027. Through the fall, EQT signaled a growing pipeline of similar opportunities, and on November 20, 2025, Homer City obtained its PA DEP Air Permit, clearing the way for full construction and reinforcing EQT’s supplier role.
