Contracted load surpasses 3.4 GW; Wisconsin PSC modifies Meta ESA; Iowa rate moratorium backdrop

2026-04-30 → 2026-06-20
By Q1 2026, Alliant signed an additional ~370 MW ESA in Iowa, taking total contracted data center demand to approximately 3.4 GW and bringing the tally of executed agreements to five. Management reiterated a quarterly cadence for disclosing ESAs and guided that near-term capacity will emphasize batteries and peakers, with up to 1.1 GW of simple-cycle CTs that could be converted to CCGTs by need, and a Q3 resource plan update reflecting MISO accreditation. Regulatory oversight sharpened. The Wisconsin PSC approved a modified ESA structure for the Beaver Dam data center (Meta), strengthening the termination charge, increasing transparency and reporting, and directing Alliant to file a stand-alone large-load tariff for future data centers. Iowa customers remain under a five-year base rate moratorium through at least late-2029, with management emphasizing that large incremental users fund infrastructure through individual customer rates. Risks flagged include large-load concentration and potential contract conversion/cancellation, and execution timing across interconnections and transmission upgrades. Management is awaiting clarity on FERC policy related to self-funded upgrades.
