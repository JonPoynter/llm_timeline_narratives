Execution, power pipeline maturation, and strategic M&A approach

2026-01-21 → 2026-06-24
Entering 2026, Prologis reported a data center power pipeline advanced to 5.7GW and signaled imminent data center starts, with roughly 40% of 2026 development planned in data centers. The firm clarified procurement stages, contemplated a dedicated data center vehicle, and reiterated execution risks tied to power, capex, and regulatory processes. Management also disclosed a 5.6GW secured/advanced pipeline by Q1'26, initiated ~$1.3B of build-to-suit data center starts in the quarter, and reported 1.3GW of LOIs representing approximately three years of activity, with disclosed DC margin targets of 25–50%. External reporting corroborated these milestones: coverage noted two Q1 data center project starts preleased to large tech firms, an additional 350MW pipeline contribution, and LOIs totaling 1.3GW. Prologis simultaneously highlighted tactical advantages (e.g., pre-ordering long-lead items) and a powered-shell-first strategy with optionality for turnkey delivery. In June, Prologis made a public, all-share approach to acquire SEGRO, linking the rationale to development and data center opportunities in the UK; SEGRO’s board rejected the proposal, triggering a UK Takeover Code deadline.
