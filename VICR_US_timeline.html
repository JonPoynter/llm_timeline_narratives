48V and Power Delivery Foundations (OCP to ORV3)

2016-03-10 → 2022-10-19
Vicor established foundational positioning in data center power delivery aligned with the industry shift to 48V distribution. Following Google’s advocacy for 48V server infrastructure at OCP in 2016, Vicor introduced 48V Direct-to-PoL components. Ecosystem partners such as Wiwynn incorporated Vicor’s Factorized Power Architecture into 48V server platforms. Vicor further advanced co-packaged power with its Power-on-Package modular current multipliers, explicitly targeting AI, ML, and high-current XPU workloads. By 2022, Meta showcased AI platforms and Open Rack v3 implementations enabling 48V power distribution and higher rack power densities, reinforcing industry momentum toward architectures that Vicor solutions address. These developments framed the market context for Vicor’s later acceleration in AI/HPC-specific vertical power delivery and licensing.

ITC Enforcement and Licensing Traction

2025-02-14 → 2025-10-20
In early 2025 the ITC issued a Final Determination confirming infringement of Vicor patents and barring importation of infringing power modules and unlicensed computing systems, with a Limited Exclusion Order and Cease and Desist Orders against named respondents. This heightened licensing urgency among OEMs and hyperscalers to avoid U.S. import risks. Vicor reported licensing traction and underscored progress toward second‑generation Vertical Power Delivery (VPD) for leading AI applications. By October 2025, Vicor stated that settlements and licenses with OEMs and hyperscalers were expected to contribute nearly $300 million to revenues through 2026, positioning IP monetization as a meaningful component of the AI/HPC power delivery opportunity.

Backlog Surge, Capacity Plans, and Licensing Upside

2026-04-21 → 2026-05-26
Vicor’s Q1’26 release highlighted a sharp demand inflection: one‑year backlog reached $301m (+75% y/y; +70% q/q) with book‑to‑bill above 2.0, led by high‑performance compute and a hyperscaler. Guidance called for Q2 revenue nearly $126m and FY’26 nearly $570m, while management detailed capacity expansions including a second 3DI line and planning for a second fab. Despite expansion, management expects capacity to remain constrained for a substantial timeframe and to be selective in customer additions. The company emphasized VPD differentiation (≈3 A/mm², up to 40× multiplication, ~1.5 mm package) and a lead HPC customer transition/ramp in H2’26. IP enforcement and licensing remain strategic with no new licenses assumed until a 2027 ITC determination, though royalties from existing deals are expected to grow modestly. On May 26, Vicor raised Q2 guidance to $142m, citing rising product revenues and royalties from an additional all‑inclusive license to its patented power system technology.
