Network-integrated AI and formal AI investment posture

2026-02-11 → 2026-03-31
On February 11, 2026, TMUS launched what it called the first real-time agentic AI platform built directly into a wireless network, starting with Live Translation for 50+ languages without apps. Coverage indicated T-Mobile uses several companies to power translation and follows a multi-model approach. The same day, management outlined AI economics and timelines in its Q4’25 release: ~$3B incremental Core Adjusted EBITDA from digitalization/AI by 2027 vs 2025, AI-RAN field trials toward late 2026 following NVIDIA lab progress, and no edge or AI-RAN revenue assumed in the 2026–2027 plan amid evolving privacy/AI regulatory risks. By mid-March, NVIDIA and TMUS showcased physical AI applications on AI-RAN-ready infrastructure using Blackwell-based servers and the NVIDIA Metropolis Blueprint, with early municipal assessment in San Jose. Separately, TMUS indicated Google One subscriptions would migrate off T-Mobile billing and support on March 31, 2026, signaling a shift in distribution for a Google service.
