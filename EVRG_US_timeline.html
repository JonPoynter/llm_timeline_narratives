Regulatory enablement and first waves of data center load

2024-12-12 → 2025-11-14
Through 2024–2025, the region saw tangible data center progress and enabling regulation. Edged opened a 26 MW Kansas City facility with Evergy participating as a utility provider. Media covered the growing energy needs of incoming Meta and Google campuses. Meta confirmed its Kansas City data center came online in August 2025. Critically, both Kansas and Missouri commissions approved Large Load Power Service (LLPS) frameworks in November 2025, establishing long-term, minimum-bill, collateralized contracts for ≥75 MW customers, designed to protect existing ratepayers. Evergy also highlighted an AI data center project by Lambda poised to scale from 24 MW to 100 MW, underscoring near-term incremental load.
