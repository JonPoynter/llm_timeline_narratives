AI-driven ABF upcycle and financing ramp

2026-03-12 → 2026-05-08
From March to May 2026, Kinsus (Kinsus Interconnect Technology Corp) signaled an aggressive pivot to capture AI-related demand in high-layer ABF substrates. Management tone and third-party reporting pointed to strong AI server cycle support, double-digit 2026 growth expectations, multi-year ABF capacity expansion through 2027, and evidence of pricing power amid persistent materials constraints. A Taiwan News/Nikkei-cited report outlined substantial greenfield expansion costs (Taoyuan ~NT$40b/US$1.25b; next-gen facility ~NT$60b) alongside a cadence of building a new fab every 2–3 years. Buy-side media coverage in late March and April asserted Kinsus had secured U.S. hyperscaler ASIC sockets—naming Google TPU and a Meta AI accelerator—with AWS Trainium shipments anticipated later in 2026. While company confirmation was not present in the filings reviewed, these reports align with the sector backdrop of unchanged CSP capex plans and tightening ABF supply. Concurrently, company commentary cited ongoing raw-material shortages and indicated ABF price increases could accelerate in 2H26. To fund capacity and working capital, the board authorized issuance of up to NT$50 billion in unsecured or sustainability bonds within a year. Filings flagged leverage/execution risks tied to deploying a potentially large program, and reiterated exposure to ABF demand/supply and mix dynamics. Kinsus did not provide backlog/RPO metrics or quantitative guidance in Q1 2026 disclosures, limiting forward-visibility in formal reporting.
