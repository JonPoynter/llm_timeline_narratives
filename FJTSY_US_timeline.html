Scaling AI delivery; Anthropic partnership and multi-model execution

2026-04-28 → 2026-06-30
In FY2025 results (released April 28, 2026), Fujitsu reported material operational adoption of AI in services delivery, with ~75% of projects using AI at least partially and targets to reach ~90% of projects and ~7.5% of processes. Management tied margin expansion to AI-based delivery platforms and modernization paths that replace legacy apps with AI agents, while flagging supply chain pressures (longer CPU/memory lead times and hardware price increases). Fujitsu also reiterated openness to alliances and continued investment in AI infrastructure, including FUJITSU‑MONAKA and inference processors, alongside a hiring pivot toward AI, quantum, and network expertise. On May 27, 2026, Fujitsu announced a strategic partnership with Anthropic, committing to deploy Claude to roughly 100,000 employees and securing early access to Anthropic’s latest models for customer solutions. The following day’s Q&A further underscored a multi-model stance: combining Anthropic and OpenAI models with Fujitsu’s Takane to maximize customer value. A June 2026 job posting reinforced the multi-provider execution capability, listing APIs across OpenAI, Anthropic, Meta Llama, and Google Gemini.

Building Takane and multi-LLM enterprise approach

2024-07-16 → 2025-09-08
Fujitsu moved to formalize enterprise-grade generative AI capabilities through a strategic partnership with Cohere and the development and launch of its own Japanese-optimized LLM, Takane. The company positioned these offerings within its Uvance and Data Intelligence PaaS portfolios. During 2024–2025, Fujitsu consistently presented a multi-model approach: enterprise frameworks that can utilize ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini; guidance to customers on choosing between NowLLM vs general-purpose LLMs; and security tooling (LLM Vulnerability Scanner and Guardrail) that supports both third-party and proprietary models. R&D advances included energy-efficient reconfiguration techniques and public release of quantized research models (e.g., Command A) on Hugging Face.

Early Azure OpenAI pilots and foundations

2023-05-08 → 2023-11-20
Fujitsu began articulating its view on generative AI and multi-model ecosystems in mid-2023, publishing thought leadership that referenced leading frontier models like GPT-3.5 and GPT-4. The company quickly moved from discourse to practical pilots using Microsoft’s Azure OpenAI Service. Through 2H CY2023, Fujitsu ran applied experiments with large Japanese enterprise customers (e.g., Mizuho Financial Group) via its Kozuchi AI platform, integrating Azure OpenAI (GPT‑3.5) and testing its own in-development LLM efforts. It also deployed Azure OpenAI (ChatGPT 3.5) into a consumer device experience (“Fukumaro”) via Fujitsu Client Computing Limited.
