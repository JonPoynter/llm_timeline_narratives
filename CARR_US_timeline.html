GenAI features and hyperscale data center demand ramp

2026-02-02 → 2026-04-30
Carrier introduced a generative AI feature, "Tell Me More," within Abound to enhance technician workflows, building on a connected base exceeding 150,000 equipment points. The 25Q4 earnings release set an ambitious ~$1.5B 2026 data center revenue target, cited strong order momentum, collaboration with NVIDIA on climate-optimized designs, and detailed a liquid-cooling CDU roadmap. Management also highlighted AI use in productivity initiatives with ~$100M of incremental 2026 investments, while noting execution risk to on-time conversion of the data center pipeline. In 26Q1, Carrier reported data center orders up >500% and a backlog fully covering expected 2026 data center sales, with capacity for incremental 2026 orders and 2027 bookings underway. QuantumLeap-related orders totaled $300–$400M to date, data center margins were accretive to CHVAC, CDU milestones advanced, and China CHVAC showed pockets of data center strength. Carrier Ventures expanded its investment in ZutaCore to scale waterless liquid cooling for AI data centers, reinforcing the strategy for high-density workloads.

Foundational cloud and AI platforming

2020-02-05 → 2024-02-06
Carrier established its foundational cloud and AI capabilities with Amazon Web Services beginning in 2020, migrating core IT workloads and selecting AWS as its preferred cloud provider while initiating co-development of the Lynx digital platform using IoT, analytics, and machine learning. These moves underpinned digitization across building and cold-chain offerings. By early 2024, Carrier’s 10-K documented continued reliance on AWS for connectivity, artificial intelligence, and machine learning, and disclosed a multi-year strategic collaboration agreement to expand SaaS solutions across HVAC performance, sustainability, and safety/security. This period cemented Carrier’s cloud backbone and data/AI enablement for later productization.
