4Q25 momentum: AI data-center and semiconductor demand lift HST

2026-02-04 → 2026-03-01
IDEX’s 4Q25 results (reported Feb 4, 2026) showed a step-up in AI-related demand, with record orders and accelerating growth in Health & Science Technologies (HST) tied to data-center power and semiconductor applications. Management highlighted minimal incremental capex needs to pursue multiple data-center-adjacent opportunities (e.g., behind-the-meter power, thermal management, optical communications), and disclosed semiconductor exposure just under 10% of IDEX (about half consumables). Alongside this positive demand signal, management cautioned on concentration risks within HST and broader uncertainties around AI industry growth, timing, and customer investment cycles (10-K filed Feb 20, 2026). The March 2026 Investor Overview positioned AI & Digitization as a key megatrend and emphasized targeted advantaged markets such as data centers, thermal management, and wafer manufacturing.

Foundational AI governance and risk framing

2025-02-20 → 2025-12-31
IDEX formally acknowledged artificial intelligence as both an internal capability under development and a source of legal, regulatory, and reputational risk in its 2024 Form 10-K filed in February 2025. Management described being in the initial stages of incorporating AI into business activities and product and service offerings while noting a rapidly evolving legal landscape in intellectual property, cybersecurity, and data protection. During this period, there were no disclosed partnerships with specific frontier model providers. The emphasis remained on governance, compliance readiness, and risk management rather than external AI model integrations or commercial AI product launches.
