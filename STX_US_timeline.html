Foundations: Cloud ingest and early AI demand signals

2022-10-19 → 2024-12-31
Seagate’s AI-adjacent posture began with data logistics integrations that positioned its storage hardware and services to move mass data into major public clouds. In October 2022, the company introduced Cloud Import for Lyve Mobile, enabling secure, accelerated transfers to Amazon S3, Google Cloud Platform, Microsoft Azure, and Lyve Cloud, underscoring Seagate’s role in the data pipeline feeding AI workloads. By mid-2024, management framed AI as a structural driver: nearline cloud revenue more than doubled y/y and Seagate articulated HDDs’ “crucial role” in AI’s adoption curve for both training data integrity and generated content retention. The company also highlighted enterprise preparation for higher-density storage as organizations ready on‑prem and private cloud capacity for future AI applications. Sustainability and data sanitization concerns remained salient, with Seagate emphasizing adherence to NIST/ISO/IEEE standards and referencing Meta’s stated priorities—highlighting persistent data security and lifecycle risks as storage volumes scale alongside AI.

Allocation through CY27, HAMR Mozaic 4+ scale-up, and portfolio focus

2026-01-01 → 2026-06-27
In 1H26 Seagate moved from qualification to scaled production of Mozaic 4+ HAMR drives, with two leading hyperscalers in production and capacities up to 44TB shipping. Management indicated nearline capacity remained fully allocated through calendar 2026 with long‑term agreements extending into 2027, and later said supply was largely allocated through CY27 with build‑to‑order finalized for FY27—providing multi‑quarter visibility but heightening execution and fulfillment risk. Financially, Seagate delivered record margin performance, strong free cash flow, and guided to rising revenue/EPS as pricing discipline, mix shift to HAMR, and cost controls expanded operating leverage. The company also divested its Lyve Cloud business to Wasabi to concentrate on core mass‑capacity storage amid the AI supercycle, preserving continuity for Lyve Cloud customers via a dedicated provider. Market reactions were swift: shares rallied on the April outlook but sold off after comments at JPMorgan TMT about the long lead-time to add factories, reflecting investor sensitivity to supply responsiveness within the AI hardware cycle.
