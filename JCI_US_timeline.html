AI infrastructure surge: cooling, modules, and AI optimization acquisitions

2026-04-27 → 2026-05-22
JCI sharpened its focus on AI infrastructure and data center demand. The company acquired Nantum AI to deepen AI-driven energy optimization in OpenBlue and signed a Global Framework Agreement with Armada to produce modular data center systems in Arizona targeting megawatt-scale AI workloads. Q2 FY2026 results underscored strong data center demand, record backlog, and an expanding CDU pipeline with ~$100M in expected FY26 revenue, while acknowledging power infrastructure constraints that may push some deliveries by 12–18 months. JCI also emphasized product development across CDUs, chillers, Metasys controls, and fire/suppression for data centers, and reiterated that liquid cooling has not reduced AHU content.

This period marks both customer-facing AI capability growth and maturing internal AI operations and guardrails.

Foundations: Cloud partnerships and edge AI build-out

2018-01-14 → 2022-06-23
Johnson Controls laid the groundwork for AI-enabled solutions through early partnerships and platform choices. In retail tech, Tyco Retail Solutions (Sensormatic) aligned with Google Cloud to power real-time analytics and modernize its digital stack, followed by the launch of Sensormatic IQ on Google Cloud and an expanded Google Cloud/JCI relationship that included migrating SAP ECC to Google Cloud. Concurrently, JCI accelerated its edge AI capabilities by acquiring FogHorn and partnering with Accenture to establish AI-enabled OpenBlue Innovation Centers using AI, digital twins, IoT, and cloud. These steps established both cloud and edge pillars for JCI’s AI roadmap across buildings and retail solutions.
