Early data center footholds

2010-02-24 → 2017-08-16
Bloom Energy’s role in powering digital infrastructure dates back more than a decade. In 2010, the company publicly highlighted marquee enterprise customers including Google, which in 2008 installed a 400 kW Bloom Energy Server to power a building on its campus that included an experimental data center. In 2017, Equinix announced what it called the largest deployment of fuel cells for the colocation data center industry at the time: a 15‑year PPA enabling more than 37 MW of Bloom fuel cells across 12 IBX facilities. These deployments established Bloom’s credibility in data center environments well before the current AI-driven power surge.

Partnerships align with AI buildout (CoreWeave, Equinix, Oracle, Brookfield)

2024-07-16 → 2025-10-13
As AI demand accelerated, Bloom announced a strategic partnership with CoreWeave in mid‑2024 to power a high‑performance data center in Illinois, positioning its fuel cells directly inside the AI compute supply chain. By early 2025, Bloom and Equinix expanded their collaboration to more than 100 MW across 19 IBX data centers, with 75 MW operational and another 30 MW under construction. In mid‑2025, Oracle selected Bloom to deliver onsite power to OCI data centers within 90 days, complementing Oracle’s broader AI push that included making xAI’s Grok models available on OCI and, separately, OpenAI’s Stargate plans to add 4.5 GW of data center capacity. CoreWeave simultaneously scaled its ties with OpenAI, culminating in $22.4B of agreements by September 2025. To finance and accelerate AI infrastructure globally, Bloom and Brookfield announced a $5B strategic partnership in October 2025.

2026 acceleration: Oracle 2.8 GW, Project Jupiter, orders and risks

2026-01-08 → 2026-06-13
In 2026 Bloom’s AI-linked momentum accelerated. In January, coverage highlighted a large AEP fuel‑cell order (~$2.65B).
