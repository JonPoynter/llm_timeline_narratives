2026-02-05 → 2026-04-30
In early 2026, MPS raised its 2026 Enterprise Data growth floor from 50%+ to an implied ~85% and increased its output capacity target from >$4B toward ~$6B, reflecting higher confidence in AI/data center demand. The company detailed optical module traction (including 1.6T ramps), continued sampling of an 800‑V data-center power solution, and near-term shipments of high-density packaging—alongside first sampling of high-speed DDR5 interface products. Management also flagged execution risk around elevated Enterprise Data growth targets and acknowledged revenue concentration/channel dynamics: the 2025 Form 10‑K disclosed significant reliance on a few third‑party distributors.
