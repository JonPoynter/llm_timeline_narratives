24/7 CFE platform and first hyperscaler decarbonization deals

2022-03-07 → 2023-06-29
Constellation launched a 24/7/365 carbon-free energy (CFE) matching solution in collaboration with Microsoft, positioning itself as a data and software-enabled clean power supplier to hyperscalers. Through 2023, Microsoft became an early customer for Constellation's hourly matching platform, with a landmark agreement to supply a Boydton, Virginia data center. The deal blends hourly nuclear environmental attributes with Microsoft’s renewable purchases to operate on nearly 100% clean power, signaling early enterprise adoption of granular CFE matching aligned with AI-driven data center growth.

Large-scale nuclear PPAs with hyperscalers (Microsoft, Meta) and restart/expansion pathways

2024-09-20 → 2025-08-27
In late 2024, Constellation announced a 20-year PPA with Microsoft that enables the launch of the Crane Clean Energy Center and the restart of Three Mile Island Unit 1, adding roughly 835 MW of carbon-free capacity to PJM by 2028. External reporting highlighted a planned $1.6 billion investment to restore key systems, contingent on regulatory approvals. In 2025, Constellation signed a 20-year, 1,121 MW PPA with Meta tied to the Clinton Clean Energy Center in Illinois, with plant uprates and community benefits. Constellation and regional stakeholders later celebrated the agreement and noted potential advanced reactor or SMR development at the Clinton site, illustrating optionality to meet rising AI-driven data center power needs. Separately, reporting showed Meta broadening nuclear procurement with other providers alongside Constellation.
