Multi‑model agentic AI scale‑up and monetization

2026-02-05 → 2026-06-30
In 2026, Atlassian accelerated an open, multi‑model AI strategy. Anthropic released Claude Opus 4.6 with new agent features, Google highlighted Atlassian as an Agent Marketplace partner at Next ’26, and Atlassian expanded its Google Cloud partnership to add Gemini 3 Flash to power agentic AI and deepen Rovo integrations with Google Workspace and Gemini Enterprise. Atlassian also advanced first‑party developer enablement with the Forge LLMs API preview, letting apps call Claude models hosted on Amazon Bedrock entirely within Atlassian’s environment. Financially, Atlassian reported strong Q3 FY26 metrics: Service Collection ARR surpassed $1B (>30% y/y), RPO reached $3.996B (+37% y/y), cloud revenue grew to $1.132B (+29% y/y), and free cash flow was $561M (31% margin). Management noted rapid growth in AI credit consumption and strong competitive displacements while flagging risk if AI adoption or consumption slows. New product entries included Rovo Service, Proactive AIOps, Rovo Dev in Jira, and the Claude Agent for Jira, which integrates Anthropic’s Managed Agents with Jira workflows. Platform dependencies were further formalized via Atlassian’s sub‑processor disclosures listing Google Vertex AI and OpenAI for Atlassian Intelligence and Rovo. Alphabet’s DeepMind moved into a new facility in London, indicative of continued investment by a key model provider.

Ecosystem connectors and agent platform expansion

In 2025, Atlassian broadened its agentic ecosystem reach. The Remote MCP Server launch, with Anthropic as the first official partner and hosted on Cloudflare, let customers securely interact with Atlassian data and perform multi‑step actions directly from Claude. By December, Atlassian introduced the Rovo MCP Connector for ChatGPT, allowing ChatGPT users to read from and write to Jira and Confluence, automating multi‑step actions and enabling writeback. These steps positioned Atlassian as a key enterprise context provider across external agent platforms, increasing distribution surface area while also creating potential platform dependency considerations.

Foundations: Atlassian Intelligence and early gen‑AI adoption

2023-04-19 → 2024-05-01
Atlassian introduced Atlassian Intelligence in April 2023, explicitly leveraging OpenAI within its cloud platform. The initiative positioned AI as a virtual teammate embedded in Jira, Confluence, and other products, accelerating drafting, summarization, and task automation by tapping the Atlassian platform and Teamwork Graph. By December 2023, the first wave of Atlassian Intelligence capabilities reached general availability across Premium and Enterprise editions with org‑level controls. Through 2023, Atlassian also highlighted a growing ecosystem of marketplace apps powered by GPT‑4/ChatGPT. In early 2024, OpenAI's Sora preview underscored the rapid frontier model evolution, and Atlassian launched Rovo in May 2024 to unify enterprise knowledge and enable agents to automate multi‑step workflows across Atlassian and third‑party tools.
