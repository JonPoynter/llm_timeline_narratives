Early AI groundwork and ecosystem alignment

2024-07-01 → 2025-12-31
SPS Commerce began publicly describing how it applies artificial intelligence to its retail supply chain network in mid-2024, highlighting use of AI on trading-partner data and citing the use of established cloud AI providers for security and governance. The company’s scale (over 120,000 customers and over $500M in 2023 revenue) positions it to embed AI into existing workflows rather than launching standalone tools. In late 2025, SPS joined industry peers to found the Commerce Operations Foundation and support the Order Network eXchange (onX), an open specification designed to move orders, inventory, and fulfillment data across commerce, logistics, and emerging AI systems. The initiative explicitly contemplates orders originating from AI assistants such as ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude, signaling SPS’s intent to interoperate with multiple model ecosystems via standards like the Model Context Protocol (MCP).
