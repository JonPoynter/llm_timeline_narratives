AI-driven demand surge; pricing actions; interposer pivot and execution milestones

2026-01-01 → 2026-06-30
Industry trackers reported tightening mature-node capacity and potential price hikes into early 2026 as AI applications boosted demand for power management and related components. VIS indicated it could not fully satisfy surging AI server/data center demand and planned NT$60–70B (US$1.9–2.22B) 2026 capex, with 85% allocated to the Singapore JV, while separately announcing across-the-board foundry price increases of 10–15% starting in April. In 26Q1 disclosures, VIS pivoted part of the Singapore 12" fab to include interposers for advanced AI packaging (30–40nm). Phase-one investment was reduced from $7.8B to $6.7B and nameplate capacity from 55k to 44k wpm by 2029. Management said first-phase capacity was fully committed via LTAs, with samples targeted for Jun–Jul 2026 and mass production in 1Q27. Execution and near-term margin pressure risks were flagged, with ECB financing supporting the heavy capex profile.

Singapore 300mm JV launched; technology licensed; governance and disclosure controls

2024-06-05 → 2025-12-31
VIS and NXP announced the VSMC joint venture to build a 300mm fab in Singapore focused on 130nm–40nm mixed-signal, power management and analog products, with an expected 55k wpm output by 2029 and a total initial build cost of $7.8B. The JV includes assured proportional capacity to both partners and will be operated by VIS. Later in June 2024, VSMC’s board approved a TSMC technology license, with a $150M fee, and reiterated plans for mass production in 2027. In January 2025, VIS’s annual report emphasized NDA requirements and structured information governance, underscoring compliance and disclosure practices—relevant to customer confidentiality as the company expands into higher-value supply chains.
