Hyperscaler ramp, Nvidia investment, supply constraints and risk management

2026-02-03 → 2026-06-27
Entering 2026, Lumentum reported surging OCS demand: by February its OCS backlog was well beyond $400 million, and it received a separate multi‑hundred‑million‑dollar CPO order deliverable in 1H 2027. In March, Bloomberg reported Nvidia would invest $2 billion into Lumentum (part of a $4 billion optics push) including purchase agreements and access rights, validating Lumentum’s role in AI‑scale optical infrastructure. At the same time, management flagged execution and supply risks. May filings noted ~$400 million of OCS shipments under control for 2H FY26, while acknowledging a “tight rope” ramp; pump laser constraints and an EML supply‑demand imbalance >30% forced allocation, though a U.S. pump capacity ramp is planned. Broader regulatory/export‑control risks were reiterated, and a June Reuters piece detailed China’s tighter InP export license regime, wafer prices up to ~$5,000, and Lumentum sold out through 2028. Lumentum increased liquidity via Series A preferred issuance, pursued LTAs/prepayments/take‑or‑pay to support constrained lines, and indicated Greensboro fab conversion could contribute from 2028 toward a >$5B CPO/ELS opportunity. A June company note highlighted hyperscaler capex (Microsoft, Amazon, Google, Meta) as a strong downstream demand signal. Management also indicated a potential competitive exception at Google due to its own architecture overlapping OCS functionality.

The company’s Annual Report highlighted the strategic rationale of its Cloud Light acquisition, emphasizing advanced optical modules and positioning to serve Cloud & Networking customers as they optimize data centers for AI/ML. Foundational photonics partnerships for AI interconnect

2022-03-09 → 2024-12-31
Lumentum established core partnerships and technology positions aimed at AI-era data center networking. In March 2022, it announced a strategic collaboration with Ayar Labs to supply CW‑WDM MSA compliant external light sources critical for optical I/O in AI, data center, and HPC applications, signaling early commitment to co-packaged optics ecosystems. By September 2024, Lumentum, alongside Marvell and Coherent, demonstrated interoperable 800G ZR/ZR+ pluggable modules suitable for 500 km data center interconnects, explicitly framed around cloud operators scaling for the AI era. These moves cemented Lumentum’s role in optical links that underpin high-capacity AI clusters.
