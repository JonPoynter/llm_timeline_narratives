Fab Next launch and AI-driven demand narrative emerges

2023-11-07 → 2024-12-31
Siltronic entered the current AI upcycle with the first wafers produced at its new Singapore fab (often referred to as Fab Next) in November 2023. Management highlighted that ramp-phase sales were substantially underpinned by long-term agreements and customer prepayments, linking capacity additions to demand tailwinds from digitalization and artificial intelligence. Through 2024, the company publicly framed AI as a structural driver of wafer demand, noting that AI servers require substantially more wafer area than conventional servers and that AI-related server shipments are expected to grow at >30% CAGR. This positioning connected Siltronic's products into the upstream of the AI compute value chain via foundries and chipmakers such as TSMC, Samsung, Intel, GlobalFoundries, Nvidia, and AMD. In late 2024, external developments like OpenAI’s in-house chip effort with Broadcom and TSMC provided additional evidence of intensifying AI hardware investment that could indirectly support wafer demand.

Q1 2026: AI demand strengthens; LTAs, cash metrics, and mixed near-term conditions

2026-01-01 → 2026-06-27
In Q1 2026, Siltronic reported AI‑driven end markets continued to strengthen. However, near‑term volumes were weighed by memory customers’ capacity constraints and elevated 200mm inventories. Management highlighted stronger-than-expected server demand but cautioned that chipmaker capacity could cap upside. The company underscored robust LTA coverage—about two‑thirds of business under LTAs and roughly 80% of Singapore capacity covered, with some contracts running to 2030—and reiterated discipline to sign new LTAs only on favorable terms. Financially, Siltronic reported customer prepayments totaling about €515m at March 31, 2026, guided 2026 capex at €180–220m, and noted Q1 free cash flow of –€89.2m alongside net financial debt of €935.5m. Pricing signals were mixed: some improvement in 300mm spot outside LTAs while 200mm remained under pressure. Operationally, Fab Next’s ramp progressed with all major customers qualified by mid‑2025 and 300mm epi added in Singapore for the first time. Risks cited included Chinese competition at smaller diameters, a ~6‑month lag for 200mm uplift due to power inventories, and the possibility of power‑related caveats affecting production cuts. Externally, AI infrastructure buildouts continued: Meta launched MTIA chips with TSMC, and Anthropic expanded compute agreements with Google and Broadcom. These developments imply sustained upstream demand for advanced wafers even as near-term mix and inventory dynamics remain a headwind.
