AI silicon customizations and GF photonics/CPO push

2026-04-06 → 2026-06-24
In 2Q26, hyperscalers accelerated custom AI silicon roadmaps: Broadcom signed a long-term agreement with Google through 2031 for custom AI chips; Meta extended its Broadcom-based custom processors deal to 2029, including an initial 1+ GW capacity commitment; and OpenAI unveiled its Jalapeño inference chip co-developed with Broadcom and taped out at TSMC. For GF, these shifts are indirect but point to sustained AI demand with potential risks if leading-edge compute remains concentrated at other foundries. GF advanced its data-center connectivity franchise. Management launched the SCALE co-packaged optics (CPO) solution targeting AI data-center connectivity; guided that silicon photonics revenue is on track to roughly double in 2026; reported Communications Infrastructure & Datacenter revenue of $230M (+32.2% y/y) in Q1’26; and highlighted ongoing talks on customer prepayments for oversubscribed corridors. Contract liabilities (prepayments under LTAs) ended Q1’26 at $1.075B, though down from year-end — flagged as a potential risk to forward commitments. Management also cautioned on geopolitical/export-control risks and noted ~50 bps per-quarter GM headwinds in 2026 from supply-chain security actions. Separately, EE Times reported GF expects to be first with silicon to support the OCI MSA, positioning its photonics and packaging stack to enable multi-vendor GPU interconnects in AI data centers.

IP posture and open-source alignment

2019-08-25 → 2022-08-03
GlobalFoundries asserted foundational IP rights in 2019 via multiple complaints across U.S. and German venues, signaling a defensive posture in core process technologies versus leading foundry competitors and downstream device makers. While historic, the breadth of parties and courts underscores GF’s long-standing effort to protect process IP critical to advanced nodes and end markets relevant to AI infrastructure. By 2022, GF complemented litigation with ecosystem openness, joining Google’s open-source silicon initiative. The release of an Apache 2.0-licensed PDK for GF’s 180MCU platform and a no-cost silicon realization program via Efabless expanded developer access and seeded future design funnels. This move aligns GF with an open innovation channel that can lower barriers for emerging AI-adjacent silicon and control-plane devices.
