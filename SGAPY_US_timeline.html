Operational AI scaling: sovereign AI, employee tools, and DC economics

2026-04-27 → 2026-05-22
In 2026, Singtel moved further into applied AI. Its RE:AI unit partnered with Mistral AI to bolster a sovereign AI offering in Singapore, leveraging Nxera data centers and RE:AI’s GPU fleet to provide multi-tenant GPUaaS and AIaaS for regulated sectors. Internally, Singtel deployed ChatGPT Enterprise to more than 3,000 employees with high monthly active usage, indicating broad-based productivity adoption of frontier tooling. Financially and operationally, FY2026/27 disclosures underscored the DC-driven AI backbone: strong pre-commitments at Nxera facilities, growing Digital InfraCo revenues, and significant capex guidance for FY2027. Management highlighted both upside from AI-related demand and downside from energy cost headwinds and execution/utilization risks tied to the multi-market DC expansion and large capex envelope. Capital structure optionality, including a potential REIT IPO and other solutions, remains under evaluation.

JV plans for Telco LLM and AI infrastructure/services expansion

2024-02-26 → 2024-08-29
At MWC Barcelona 2024, Singtel and GTAA peers held their inaugural meeting and announced plans to establish a joint venture to develop telco-specific LLMs, with Singtel’s CEO indicating intent to deploy outputs across the Group. By June 2024, the JV agreement was formally signed with equal investments from founding parties, targeting a collective reach of roughly 1.3 billion customers across 50 countries. Concurrently, Singtel advanced its AI infrastructure and service portfolio. It expanded GPU-as-a-Service beyond Singapore into Southeast Asia via Bridge Alliance, and at its Digital InfraCo Investor Day, showcased "Models as a Service" including Llama 2 and Mistral, aligning enterprise offerings with multi-model AI stacks.

Alliance formation and early AI partnerships

2023-07-27 → 2023-12-07
Singtel entered the public AI narrative through industry collaboration. In July 2023, it joined SK Telecom, Deutsche Telekom, and e& to create the Global Telco AI Alliance (GTAA), signing a multilateral MOU to co-develop a Telco AI Platform and explore joint AI services. This positioned Singtel alongside major global carriers to accelerate AI transformation in core telco operations and customer experience. By October 2023, GTAA members SK Telecom and Deutsche Telekom announced plans to jointly build a telco-specific LLM with collaboration from Anthropic (Claude 2) and Meta (Llama 2), signaling concrete model-development pathways that could benefit Alliance members, including Singtel. In December 2023, Singtel’s ICT arm NCS deepened its AI posture by forming a strategic partnership with Google Cloud to drive AI-led transformation for public and private sector clients across APAC, aligning delivery capabilities with hyperscaler AI stacks.
