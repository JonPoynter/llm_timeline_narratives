2026 Q1: Backlog growth, cash strength, and AI/data center tailwinds

2026-04-10 → 2026-06-30
Into 2026, market commentary cited grid upgrades and AI data center loads as supportive for MYR Group. On its Q1 2026 release, the company reported record backlog of $2.84bn (+7.7% y/y) and emphasized strong demand linked to data centers across C&I and T&D. Management also raised organic growth outlook to roughly 12% for 2026 and increased segment margin targets, while noting master service agreement (MSA) backlog recognition limitations (rolling ~90 days) and risks tied to potential data center slowdowns or permitting delays. Operationally, the firm remained in a net cash position with $460.5m revolver availability, and generated $84.7m operating cash flow and $68.6m free cash flow in Q1. The May 2026 investor presentation reiterated that data center activity—driven by AI demand—was a key factor underpinning momentum and opportunity set.
