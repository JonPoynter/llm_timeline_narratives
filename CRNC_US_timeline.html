FY26 Q2: LLM-driven connected growth, xUI traction, backlog and legal actions

2026-05-07 → 2026-05-07
In its Q2 FY26 release, Cerence cited momentum from xUI-powered vehicles entering production and broader adoption of Audio AI and generative AI across OEMs. BYD became the first OEM shipping xUI-equipped vehicles, with additional OEM SOPs anticipated in June–July 2026. Management reported five wins out of six xUI bids to date and targeted potentially six out of seven by quarter-end; xUI PPUs run above current rates with revenue mix shifting more into FY27. Connected services remained the near-term growth engine, aided by LLM-based features such as Chat Pro. The company reported TTM Adjusted Total Billings of $238.8M, RPO of $235.6M, a five-year backlog update of $971.3M, and deferred revenue of $198.6M. It guided to ~$10M in Q3 fixed-license signings and ~$23M fixed-license revenue for FY26, with a five-year backlog target around ~$1B and connected services revenue of $15.31M (+21% y/y). Cerence also highlighted ongoing IP matters (Apple, Sony, TCL, Amazon) and filed an ITC complaint against Amazon, while generating $13.63M in free cash flow for the quarter.

2025: Hybrid agentic platform xUI and multi-model architecture

2024: Microsoft/OpenAI collaboration and VW Group deployments of Chat Pro

2024-01-03 → 2024-10-30
Cerence and Microsoft announced a collaboration to bring OpenAI’s ChatGPT to vehicles via Azure OpenAI Service, and Volkswagen Group became the first OEM to adopt Cerence Chat Pro, an automotive-grade ChatGPT integration. Deployments broadened through 2024 across VW, Škoda, and Audi, including a cloud-update rollout in Europe and expansion to the U.S., with Audi adoption corroborated by both Cerence and Audi MediaCenter releases. Renault later partnered to integrate generative AI into its Reno assistant. These milestones demonstrate rapid OEM adoption of LLM-powered features for enhanced in-car conversational experiences, often leveraging Cerence’s platform as a mediated, automotive-grade layer over OpenAI models.
