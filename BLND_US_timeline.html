Autopilot launch, MCP server opening platform to AI agents, and 26Q1 traction

2026-03-03 → 2026-06-16
Blend launched Autopilot in March 2026 as the first agent of Intelligent Origination to automate real‑time compliance reviews and borrower follow‑ups. In April–May, Blend shipped and publicly launched the Autopilot MCP Server—built on the Model Context Protocol introduced by Anthropic—opening its platform to lender‑ and partner‑built agents with programmatic access across credit, underwriting, compliance, disclosures, and closing. Borrower chat and pricing/rate‑lock alignment were also introduced as the MCP platform hardened. Q1 FY2026 results (reported May 7) were ahead of guidance, with management attributing performance to execution and early Autopilot/agent‑first traction. The company emphasized a free‑trial period through Q2 with no Autopilot revenue assumed in guidance, while noting an Autopilot pipeline of about $10 million and outlining a monetization path from free, to flat‑fee, then per‑funded‑loan. Management highlighted rapid self‑serve adoption by large financial institutions, improving conversion and cycle‑time reductions, and signaled 2026 AI budgets at large banks had ‘flipped on.’ Integrations continued, including an expanded partnership with MeridianLink to streamline data sharing across origination systems.
