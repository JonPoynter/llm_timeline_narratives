Nuclear PPAs with AWS and Meta; early data center siting next to Comanche Peak

2025-09-29 → 2026-02-27
In late 2025, Vistra pivoted from renewables to long-duration nuclear for hyperscaler load. On September 29, 2025, the company announced a 20-year, 1,200 MW carbon‑free PPA from Comanche Peak with a large investment grade customer (subsequently identified by management and trade press as AWS), with initial delivery slated for late 2027 and full ramp by 2032. On January 9, 2026, Vistra and Meta unveiled 20-year PPAs totaling 2,609 MW across Perry, Davis‑Besse, and Beaver Valley, including 433 MW of uprates, with deliveries beginning in late 2026 and full online by 2034. Around the same time, reports surfaced of Amazon’s proposed $5 billion data center campus on Vistra property adjacent to Comanche Peak, indicating direct siting coordination between load and generation. Subsequent company communications and industry coverage reiterated schedules, scale, and optionality for new nuclear development.
