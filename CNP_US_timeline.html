Q1’26 load surge, ERCOT filings, and AI operations platformization

2026-04-23 → 2026-06-01
On April 23, 2026, CenterPoint reported 12.2 GW of firmly committed large-load demand in Greater Houston, including 3.5 GW under construction, and a forecast to energize 8 GW of data center load by 2029. Management described plans to file roughly 9 GW of additional projects with ERCOT within weeks, while press coverage noted 3.2 GW had already received ERCOT approval. The company reiterated that customer interconnection facilities are customer‑funded, limiting direct capex exposure, and highlighted demand-charge economics per GW of added load. Concurrent financing and planning updates reaffirmed a ~$65.5B 10‑year capital plan and a ~$6.8B 2026 capex plan, while also noting negative free cash flow in Q1 given seasonal capex. Operationally, CenterPoint advanced its AI-enabled operations with Technosylva’s integrated planning and operations platform to improve outage forecasting and restoration.

AI-enabled grid resilience partnerships

2024-10-02 → 2025-06-25

In October 2024 the company announced agreements with Neara and Technosylva to deploy AI-powered infrastructure modeling and predictive analytics across its 5,000‑square‑mile Greater Houston service area and the broader multi-state network. The Technosylva collaboration was framed within a $21 billion five‑year resilience investment plan. By mid-2025, CenterPoint broadened its AI ecosystem. It adopted Climavision’s Horizon AI Forecasting Suite to strengthen high‑resolution weather forecasting, and convened a Resiliency Technology Summit highlighting strategic relationships with seven AI and data analytics providers, including Palantir and Urbint, to enhance situational awareness and emergency response.
