FY26 execution and integrations amid AI cycle volatility (May–June 2026)

2026-05-01 → 2026-06-30
In May 2026, Infineon’s Q2 FY2026 update emphasized rising AI data‑center power demand: the company reiterated AI power revenue targets (~€1.5B FY2026 and ~€2.5B FY2027), lifted guidance, and outlined ~€2.7B FY2026 investments focused on Dresden M4 and capacity aligned to AI power. Management flagged tightening supply, potential allocation in AI‑related products, and very high 300‑mm utilization. Backlog climbed to about €25B with some orders confirmed into the next fiscal year, though management warned that outcomes hinge on AI infrastructure spending, FX, and execution on the capacity ramp. Concurrently, Infineon deepened ecosystem ties around AI infrastructure: it joined NVIDIA’s MGX AI Factory power ecosystem and integrated its OPTIGA TPM with NVIDIA Jetson Thor to address security for Physical AI/robotics platforms. Market volatility around AI semiconductors punctuated June trading, while Infineon content also surfaced in Google-aligned device ecosystems (Find My network accessories; ChromeOS devices with Infineon TPMs).
