Interoperability R&D, AI infrastructure scaling, and expanded Gemini go-to-market

2026-04-22 → 2026-06-24
In 2026, NTT advanced research for cross-model operability, unveiling a framework for lossless token vocabulary reduction enabling shared vocabularies and heterogeneous LLM coordination. Commercially, NTT DATA expanded its Google Cloud collaboration, forming a Gemini Enterprise practice targeting 5,000 certifications and up to 500 AI agents, and began Japanese license provisioning for Gemini Enterprise and Workspace. To fund broader group needs amid heavy AI-infrastructure investment, NTT marketed a multi-currency offshore bond deal around $10 billion.

Building multi-provider AI go-to-market and tsuzumi footprint

2023-11-16 → 2025-10-20
NTT Group began institutionalizing enterprise AI offerings through NTT DATA, initially centering on Microsoft 365 Copilot solutions that leverage OpenAI GPT-4. In 2024, NTT DATA announced that its in-house lightweight LLM tsuzumi would be available on Microsoft Azure's Model-as-a-Service platform and set an ambition for tsuzumi revenues to reach JPY 100 billion by FY2027. NTT DOCOMO expanded consumer- and SMB-facing distribution with Supernova's multi-model "Stella AI" across owned media and select shops, featuring Gemini, ChatGPT, Claude, and tsuzumi, and image generators like Imagen3 and DALL-E3. In 2025, NTT DATA formalized a global strategic tie-up with OpenAI, becoming OpenAI's first authorized distributor of ChatGPT Enterprise in Japan starting May 2025 and targeting cumulative OpenAI-related revenues of JPY 100 billion by FY2027. The firm simultaneously deepened a Google Cloud partnership aimed at agentic AI for enterprises and launched collaboration with Mistral AI on private AI. By October 2025, NTT advanced its tsuzumi roadmap with "tsuzumi 2" performance and cost documentation, positioning it competitively on Japanese-language tasks and cost-to-serve.
