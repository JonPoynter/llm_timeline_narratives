Android Auto access dispute and regulatory remedy

2019-05-17 → 2021-05-13
Enel’s mobility app ambitions encountered platform‑access friction with Google’s Android Auto. In May 2019, the Italian Competition Authority (AGCM) opened an investigation into Google following allegations it refused interoperability for Enel X’s charging app on Android Auto, highlighting dependency on a gatekeeper platform for distribution to drivers. In May 2021, AGCM fined Google over €100 million and ordered inclusion of Enel X’s app in Android Auto and the provision of tools to ensure interoperability. The case underscored both the risks of platform control to Enel’s customer reach and the potential for regulatory remedies to restore access.

Early hyperscaler offtakes and project financing

2016-04-08 → 2018-03-19
Enel began building large-scale wind capacity in the United States supported by long‑term corporate PPAs with leading technology firms. In April 2016, Enel started constructing the 400 MW Cimarron Bend wind farm, backed by two 200 MW long‑term PPAs, including one with Google. This established a direct commercial relationship with a frontier AI provider’s parent company and tied Enel’s growth to data‑center demand. In September 2016, Enel secured approximately $500 million in tax equity financing for Cimarron Bend, further de‑risking project execution. By March 2018, Enel expanded corporate offtake exposure by signing agreements with Facebook (Meta) and Adobe for the 320 MW Rattlesnake Creek wind farm, enabling 100% renewable power for Facebook’s Papillion data center over time. Collectively these deals seeded Enel’s data center power exposure with long‑duration, contracted demand from hyperscale tech customers.
