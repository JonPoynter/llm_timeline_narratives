Foundational Google Cloud migration and GPU modernization

2022-10-06 → 2023-12-12
AppLovin deepened its infrastructure relationship with Google Cloud, expanding usage and migrating legacy workloads. In October 2022, Google Cloud disclosed that AppLovin would more than double its use of Google infrastructure and compute, following a 2021 migration of seven data centers—including five in a single day—onto Google Cloud infrastructure. The modernization stack spans Cloud CDN on Premium Network, Cloud Armor, Load Balancer, Dataproc and Google Kubernetes Engine (GKE). By December 2023, AppLovin highlighted GPU upgrades on Google Cloud’s G2 VMs powered by NVIDIA L4 GPUs, reporting up to a 4x performance improvement and as much as 40% better price/performance. The company emphasized improved automation, reduced training time, and the agility to launch in new regions at will, positioning its Axon AI advertising engine and creative pipeline for scale.
