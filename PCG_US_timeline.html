Foundational AI deployments and early data-center momentum

2024-11-13 → 2025-06-06
PG&E initiated visible, operational AI deployments and positioned its grid for growing AI-driven electricity demand. In November 2024, the company announced the first on-site generative AI deployment at a U.S. nuclear power plant at Diablo Canyon, implemented with Atomic Canyon and NVIDIA’s AI platform. In parallel, PG&E highlighted its use of AI/ML for wildfire monitoring and situational awareness, expanding an extensive network of AI-enabled cameras and weather stations. By late May 2025, PG&E quantified accelerating data-center load growth tied to AI and cloud demand, citing 8.7 GW over the next decade and linking every 1 GW of new demand to potential 1–2% customer bill reductions. The company also advanced grid capacity in San Jose through a Smart Wires project expected to unlock more than 100 MW at Los Esteros substation, with CAISO projecting up to 500 MW of incremental area load.
