IPO, First Public Earnings, AWS Partnership, and Scaling/Risk Posture

2026-05-01 → 2026-06-25
Cerebras completed a blockbuster IPO in May 2026, followed by its first earnings release as a public company in late June. The company disclosed a multi-year OpenAI agreement to deploy 750MW of inference capacity valued at over $20B, and reported RPO of $25.0B largely tied to the OpenAI MRA, with a recognition schedule extending through 2028 and beyond. Q1 showed rapid cloud revenue growth and rising cloud mix, alongside robust cash balances but negative free cash flow due to capital intensity. Cerebras also unveiled a multi-year AWS partnership for a disaggregated inference architecture (Trainium for prefill, CS‑3 for decode) with expected revenue contribution from 2027. Management flagged constraints around data centers, power, and site additions across regions. Meanwhile, OpenAI announced testing of “Jalapeño,” its first custom chip with Broadcom, indicating potential long-term substitution risk even as OpenAI cited ongoing use of Cerebras for inference.
