CPO expansion and 26Q1 hyperscaler concentration/risks

2026-01-28 → 2026-04-30
In early 2026, GUC expanded co-packaged optics initiatives via a strategic partnership with Lightmatter, combining photonic interconnect (Passage) with GUC’s ASIC and advanced packaging expertise to alleviate AI interconnect bottlenecks. On April 23, 2026, GUC demonstrated a 12 Gbps HBM4 IP platform on TSMC N3 at TSMC’s North America Technology Symposium, aligning with the advanced-node memory roadmap for AI/HPC. The 26Q1 disclosure underscored acceleration in cloud and advanced-node programs: Cloud reached 81% of revenue, 3nm and below accounted for 57%, and U.S. revenue share was 68%. However, concentration and execution risks were explicit: heavy reliance on hyperscalers/U.S. demand, an inventory build (NT$13.74bn; 131 days), and a long-term high-end packaging capacity guarantee deposit (US$37.5m through 2035) with potential utilization risk.
