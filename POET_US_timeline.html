Strategic collaborations, Lumilens $50M initial PO, financing and execution risks

2026-03-16 → 2026-06-08
POET announced strategic collaborations with LITEON and Lessengers to co-develop next-generation modules including a 1.6T 2×DR4 design for AI clusters, with prototypes targeted for late 2026 and high-volume production anticipated in 2027. On May 14, 2026, POET and Lumilens formalized a supply agreement with an initial $50 million purchase order and a stated $500+ million five-year potential, aligned with wafer-level photonic integration roadmaps. Concurrently, POET closed a $400 million registered direct equity offering on May 18, 2026, bolstering liquidity to $429.14 million as of March 31, 2026. The company also disclosed the April 27, 2026 cancellation of all Celestial AI purchase orders, and in filings highlighted multiple execution risks including dependency on product qualification and Malaysia manufacturing ramp. Market reaction saw substantial volatility into early June following the Lumilens deal and financing.

Foundational AI photonics positioning and early customer traction

2020-10-22 → 2023-11-06
POET established its manufacturing and commercialization footprint for optical engines via the Super Photonics JV with Sanan IC in 2020, explicitly targeting high-growth AI and hyperscale data center markets. This period focused on building a channel to market for its Optical Interposer-based engines and laying out how AI workloads would drive demand for higher-speed optical interconnects. Key early adoption came from Celestial AI with a 2022 supply agreement and initial purchase order for advanced light engine modules, followed by POET’s April 2023 launch of Starlight for AI applications under a co-development agreement with Celestial AI. Throughout 2023, third-party and company materials tied POET’s optical engines and light sources to AI use cases (including ChatGPT-era bandwidth needs), while management communicated plans to move into 800G and 1.6T modules to serve AI data center demand in 2024.
