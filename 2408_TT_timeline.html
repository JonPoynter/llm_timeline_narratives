Customer-financed supply security; AI memory revenue starts; ramp and execution risks

2026-04-08 → 2026-06-27
Nanya completed a large private placement on April 8, 2026, with four strategic customers (Solidigm, Sandisk, Kioxia, Cisco) to secure DRAM supply for cloud AI applications. The placement totaled about US$2.5 billion (NT$78.72bn) with a ~10.19% combined post-placement ownership, three-year lock-up, and multi-year supply arrangements. The company subsequently cited around US$5.8 billion in cash to fund expansion. Operationally, customized AI UltraWIO memory began contributing initial revenue, while DDR5 accounted for about 10% of revenue. The new fab remained on track for equipment move-in in Q1’27 with EUV capability development on plan. Management emphasized DDR5 scale readiness but also flagged risks around DDR5 mix/cost execution and the new fab/EUV ramp. Industry-wide, executives warned of bottlenecks and sustained DRAM tightness, and Nanya projected robust AI-driven bit growth with planned R&D and capacity increases.
