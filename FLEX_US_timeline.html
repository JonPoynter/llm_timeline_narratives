CPI spin-off planning and AI data center ramp

2026-05-06 → 2026-06-08
Flex announced plans to spin off its Cloud & Power Infrastructure (CPI) segment into a separate public company targeted to close in Q1 calendar 2027. Management tied the move and growth acceleration to an architectural shift in AI data centers, citing a multiyear contract with Google and wins across hyperscalers, neoclouds, colos, and utilities. CPI capacity and backlog were described as booked out for the next couple of years, with programs extending into FY2029, while margin trajectories differentiate between Cloud and Power sub-segments. Capital allocation plans include FY2027 capex of $1.4–$1.6B focused on scaling CPI capacity and automation, underpinned by deferred revenue and customer advances of $2.16B. CPI delivered $1.8B in Q4 FY2026 revenue and $6.6B for FY2026, and FY2027 company revenue guidance was set at $32.3–$33.8B, reflecting continued AI/data center infrastructure demand. The company also acquired EP² to strengthen its power utility portfolio and outlined leadership for SpinCo and RemainCo. Subsequent index actions added Flex to the S&P 500 effective June 22, 2026.
