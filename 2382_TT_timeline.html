26Q1 ramp: AI racks mix surge, platform transition, and capacity/capex scale-up

2026-05-14 → 2026-05-14
On its 26Q1 release, Quanta detailed a sharp AI mix shift: servers exceeded 80% of revenue and AI servers were roughly 75% of server revenue. Shipments of >US$3m/rack high‑density AI cabinets more than doubled quarter‑on‑quarter. GB300 became the main shipping platform, with the next‑generation platform scheduled to enter volume in late Q3 and contribute in Q4. Management guided AI order visibility into 2027 (and in some remarks to 2028), reiterated triple‑digit AI revenue growth for 2026 and double‑digit sequential growth in Q2, and raised the 2026 general‑purpose server outlook to double‑digit year‑over‑year growth. The company is targeting a doubling of AI capacity by year‑end 2026 versus 2025, planning ~NT$30bn 2026 capex focused on U.S. and Thailand; Q1 capex was about NT$8.1bn. Risks flagged included margin dilution from the rapid ramp/transition and very high‑ASP racks, elevated memory costs under buy–sell arrangements (with a 2H26 shift to consignment to remove those components from revenue), supply tightness in GPUs/memory/power/cooling, execution risk on capacity ramp, and hyperscaler concentration/normalization.
