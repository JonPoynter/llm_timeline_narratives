AI server capacitor surge, program reset/succession, and capacity expansions

2026-01-01 → 2026-06-30
In FY2025–FY2027 disclosures (released April 30, 2026), Murata reported a sharp acceleration in AI/data-center MLCC demand: sales growth of roughly 85–90% y/y and guidance suggesting “doubling-like” trajectories over the next 2–3 years, supported by stronger Q4 orders tied to clearer AI server counts and early buys. Pricing held firm in data-center MLCCs with no requests for price cuts and ASP uplift from mix/miniaturization. Management targeted sustained high utilization (~90–95%) and asserted ability to meet smartphone/auto commitments while ramping servers. Capacity overlaps with auto are limited mainly to higher-voltage server MLCCs. A hyperscaler power-module first program was dropped near mass production due to a firmware issue/short life, but a larger successor with the same customer was set to start around June, with 2–3 certified suppliers. Murata prioritized capacitor and power-module growth with additional capex of about ¥80bn across FY2026–FY2027 and another ¥80bn across FY2027–FY2028. Markets reacted positively, with shares up nearly 14% on May 29, 2026 alongside AI component strength. The company also sought AI/ML talent with multi-model LLM fluency, including OpenAI, Llama, Gemini, and Claude.

Enterprise GenAI enablement and DX

2023-01-01 → 2024-06-30
Murata began formal enterprise use of generative AI via Microsoft’s Azure OpenAI Service, launching a trial of “Murata Copilot powered by ChatGPT” for roughly 30,000 domestic employees to improve productivity in development and document work. The service was run within a private Azure environment with data protection controls. By early 2024, Murata’s DX leadership reported company-provided access to ChatGPT as part of a broader program to democratize IT usage, strengthen cloud utilization, and enhance security. These steps institutionalized GenAI usage patterns across the organization.

Edge-AI hardware collaboration and recognition

2020-01-06 → 2022-12-31
Murata established an early position in edge AI by partnering with Google to package the Edge TPU into the Coral Accelerator Module, targeting on-device inference in space- and power-constrained deployments. This collaboration underscored Murata’s capability to miniaturize AI accelerators and integrate them into robust modules suitable for various industries. In 2022, Murata’s edge AI modules garnered industry recognition, winning the CEATEC AWARD 2022 Key Technology Grand Prix for two edge AI modules, including a variant featuring Google’s Edge TPU. These milestones anchored Murata’s role in enabling AI at the device edge, complementary to the later surge in data-center demand.
