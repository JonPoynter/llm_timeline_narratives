Data center ramp, execution risks, and new platforms (XR, AR) with strategic M&A

2026-02-04 → 2026-06-25
In early 2026, Qualcomm’s earnings reiterated a multibillion-dollar data-center opportunity beginning 2027, with management indicating HUMAIN shipments and AI250 execution. However, handset end-markets faced near-term headwinds from DRAM supply redirecting to HBM for AI, capping Q2 FY26 handsets around $6B and making the outlook highly dependent on memory availability. The company also completed the Alphawave IP acquisition to accelerate data-center expansion and emphasized platform qualifications across memory vendors. On April 29, Qualcomm guided to initial shipments of a custom data-center product to a leading hyperscaler in December 2026, framing the engagement as margin-accretive and flexible across merchant/custom approaches, while acknowledging execution risks in this new category. Through June, Qualcomm broadened its AI hardware and software footprint, unveiling the Snapdragon Reality Elite XR platform and START toolkit, seeing customer traction like Snap’s consumer AR ‘Specs’, investing in NEURA Robotics’ large Series C, announcing the all-stock acquisition of AI software firm Modular (~$3.9B), and disclosing at Investor Day a multi-generation Meta deal for the Dragonfly C1000 CPU with first-gen production in 2H 2028 and long-term non-handset targets that include ~$15B data-center AI revenue by FY2029.

On-device AI enablement and model ecosystem build-out

2023-07-18 → 2024-10-25
From mid-2023 through 2024, Qualcomm concentrated on enabling on-device generative AI across phones, PCs, XR, and automotive. It publicly aligned with key model ecosystems from Meta and Google, and launched developer infrastructure to simplify deploying models on Snapdragon platforms. Qualcomm and Meta announced work to optimize Llama 2 for on-device use starting in 2024, while Google’s foundation models were brought to Snapdragon 8 Gen 3. Qualcomm also expanded developer tooling via the Qualcomm AI Hub (with 75+ optimized models) and documented heterogeneous computing approaches running OpenAI’s Whisper and Meta’s Llama 2-7B locally. Meta’s Llama 3 added hardware support for Qualcomm, and Qualcomm partnered with Meta and Ollama on quantized Llama 3.2 for AI PCs. In automotive, Qualcomm and Google highlighted a multi-year collaboration involving Gemini Nano and the Snapdragon Digital Chassis. Mistral AI additionally noted support for running its models locally on Snapdragon.
