Q3 FY2026 surge, raised guidance, and execution/capacity updates

2026-05-14 → 2026-06-27
On May 14, 2026, Forgent reported continued acceleration across data center and grid end-markets with record quarterly bookings/backlog, raising FY2026 guidance. Backlog reached $1.98B with Q3 bookings of $867M and a 2.3x book-to-bill. Operating cash flow turned positive at $29M despite ongoing capacity investments, and the company indicated it was fully booked against its Q4 plan with backlog visibility extending into 2028. Management detailed capacity expansion: quarterly capex of $28M and an overall ~$205M program expected to be meaningfully complete by fiscal year-end—supporting up to ~$5B annual revenue capacity. Additional highlights included rising average order sizes, behind-the-meter opportunities amid 5–7 year interconnection timelines, and modest quick-ship flow revenue. Risks cited included execution ramp/absorption, commodity availability and pricing, backlog conversion/cancellation, and customer concentration. Separately, management disclosed a >$100M Powertrain Solutions order for the first building on a planned multi-gigawatt data center campus, reinforcing momentum with new cloud customers. The concurrent 10-Q reiterated AI and cloud computing as core demand drivers.
