Japan hyperscaler foothold: land sale and first tenant commitments

2019-05-29 → 2021-10-07
Goodman established an early hyperscaler foothold in Japan by selling land within Goodman Business Park (Inzai, Greater Tokyo) to Google in May 2019 for the company’s first data center in Japan. This positioned the precinct as a technology hub and anchored future demand from cloud providers. By October 2021, Goodman deepened its data center strategy with a development-and-long-term-lease arrangement for two Tokyo facilities with ST Telemedia Global Data Centres (STT GDC), totaling 60MW. Goodman would deliver shell-and-core and lease to STT GDC, indicating a repeatable model with hyperscale and colocation operators.

Global DC program scale-up and power banking

2026-02-19 → 2026-02-19
Goodman outlined substantial scaling of its global data center development program, highlighting an A$14b European data center development partnership, expectations to have ~0.5GW of projects underway by June 2026, and plans for WIP to approach A$18b. The Group reported that data centers account for more than 70% of WIP, with yields cited as over 8%, and indicated a preference for fully-fitted builds that can earn higher yields than core-shell. Management emphasized a 6.0GW data center power bank (3.6GW secured, 2.4GW in advanced procurement), and noted contracting across the initial ~0.5GW was largely locked or nearing final negotiation. While supported by partnership liquidity and equity commitments, Goodman flagged execution risks: grid connection and power contracting timelines, a relatively low pre-commit ratio (~40%), and exposure to third-party build mix could defer development earnings or leasing outcomes.
