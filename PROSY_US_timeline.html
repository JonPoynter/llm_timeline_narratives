Strategy articulation and AI-first operating model

2025-01-01 → 2025-12-31
Prosus publicly framed its AI strategy around the application layer, emphasizing ecosystems built atop foundational models. The company’s venture arm led new AI investments (e.g., Luzia), reinforcing exposure to consumer AI assistants. In November 2025, management announced a group-wide AI-first operating model, reported the deployment of over 20,000 agents under its Large Commerce Model (LCM), and flagged both opportunities and risks from rapid AI advances. Prosus also previewed deeper strategy updates and an acceleration plan in India, aiming for the region to lead group deployments, while acknowledging limited disclosure on AI spend and KPIs.

Foundations and early portfolio AI adoption

2023-10-20 → 2024-12-31
Prosus’s AI exposure initially surfaced through portfolio company usage and experimentation. Spanish AI assistant Luzia demonstrated significant traction in late 2023 using a mix of frontier and open-source models (GPT and Llama among others), highlighting multilingual consumer AI demand across Prosus’s target markets. In 2024, Prosus’s food-delivery affiliate iFood showcased heavy AI use on AWS (Bedrock and SageMaker) at scale, and by October 2024 began piloting OpenAI’s Realtime API (GPT‑4o) with support from Prosus’s AI team to power a voice-enabled courier assistant (“Jhow”). These implementations established real-world customer experience and operational uplift from generative AI across the group’s ecosystem.
