Digital twin, flexible AI compute trials, and FY27–31 build-out plan

2026-01-29 → 2026-06-11
National Grid launched Triton, a digital twin and data visualization platform developed with Atos to accelerate network planning, claiming up to 70% reduction in analysis time. In parallel, a UK-first live trial with Emerald AI, EPRI, Nebius, and NVIDIA demonstrated real-time, non-disruptive power flexibility of an AI cluster, validating data centers as grid-responsive assets. On May 14, 2026, management issued FY26 results and a multi-year plan for FY27–FY31: at least £70bn cumulative capex, with substantial regulatory coverage and supply chain contracting. The plan embeds significant AI/data center demand, highlights acceleration measures to bridge constraints, and flags execution risks around delivery capacity, permitting, and inflation. Additional initiatives included internal policies for AI meeting summaries and an Ofgem-funded Alpha-phase collaboration (FoSMo) to develop a shared foundational AI model for grid asset management with multiple UK operators.
