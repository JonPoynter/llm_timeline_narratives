Foundation: Edge AI model enablement and partnerships

2023-08-16 → 2024-02-21
MediaTek began formalizing its edge AI strategy through partnerships with model providers and platform enablers. In August 2023, the company announced a co-creation program with Baidu focused on adapting ERNIE/PaddlePaddle models across MediaTek hardware via NeuroPilot. Days later, MediaTek publicly aligned with Meta’s Llama 2 to accelerate on-device generative AI across smartphones and broader edge devices. By early 2024, MediaTek moved from announcements to demonstrations and optimization. At MWC 2024, it showed on-device Llama 2 running on Dimensity 9300/8300 using APU acceleration and confirmed a parallel track with Google to integrate and optimize Gemini Nano for these chipsets via the NeuroPilot toolkit, positioning its flagship and premium SoCs as multi-model, on-device AI platforms.

Data center ASIC ramp, risk guardrails, and AI device co-development

2026-04-27 → 2026-06-02
In 26Q1 disclosures, MediaTek sharpened its data center roadmap, indicating its first hyperscaler AI accelerator ASIC remains on schedule, with a ~$2B revenue contribution expected in Q4’26, a second accelerator design underway, and a raised 2027 DC ASIC TAM of ~$70–80B with a 10–15% share ambition. Management highlighted rack-level discussions but reiterated 2026–2027 growth is purely ASIC, with all DC projects OM/EPS accretive. The company cautioned that any schedule slip or yield issue could impair the Q4’26 contribution and multi‑billion trajectory into 2027, and noted advanced-node supply allocation uncertainty. Concurrently, ecosystem moves accelerated at the device edge. Nvidia and MediaTek co-announced the RTX Spark Windows PC SoC aimed at agentic AI PCs, with OEM systems anticipated in fall 2026. Rumors surfaced around OpenAI exploring a smartphone with potential MediaTek silicon involvement; subsequent reporting suggested MediaTek could be the sole supplier, though this remains unconfirmed.
