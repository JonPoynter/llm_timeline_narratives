Dragonfly G5 launch, HBM4 enablement, and strategic Rigaku stake

2026-02-19 → 2026-06-27
In early 2026, Onto capitalized on AI-driven advanced packaging demand with a multi-year volume purchase agreement over $240 million with a leading HBM manufacturer and posted record FY2025 revenue. The company launched the Dragonfly G5 platform with machine learning algorithms and reported commitments for double-digit orders, including selection for HBM4 2D inspection. A subsequent April qualification update set initial G5 shipments for June and noted AI-enhanced software and advanced algorithms. The Q1 2026 release added details: Dragonfly G5 was qualified at leading 2.5D logic and HBM customers; Atlas G6 was selected by a second logic customer for GAA metrology; advanced nodes grew 13% q/q and are expected to grow ~25% for the year; management outlined >50% advanced packaging growth in 2026 and a roughly equal 2.5D/HBM split. Onto also announced a planned 27% (~$710M) strategic equity stake in Rigaku, with expected dividends (~$7M annually) and licensing economics targeted to offset foregone interest within 12 months of close, alongside product scope notes (X-ray complementary to OCD/EchoScan) and portfolio TAM signals (panel-level packaging ~$200M over several years, ramping 2027). Risks noted included slightly extended lead times (no material bottlenecks), reliance on advanced nodes and HBM demand, and lack of disclosed backlog/RPO metrics.
