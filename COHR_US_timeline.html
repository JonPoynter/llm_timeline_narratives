US InP capacity expansion and external supply‑chain headwinds

2026-06-10 → 2026-06-27
In mid‑June 2026, external reporting highlighted tightening Chinese export licensure on indium phosphide substrates, with wafer prices up sharply and select suppliers sold out through 2028, posing a supply‑chain headwind to global AI datacenter optics. Against this backdrop, Coherent announced a U.S. CHIPS Act letter of intent for up to $50 million to expand its Sherman, Texas 6‑inch InP manufacturing facility, aiming to double production space and quadruple wafer capacity to strengthen domestic supply for AI networking technologies and deepen its NVIDIA partnership.

NVIDIA partnership and FY26 Q3 AI optics ramp

2026-03-02 → 2026-05-06
Beginning March 2, 2026, Coherent entered a multiyear strategic agreement with NVIDIA that included a $2 billion equity investment and multibillion‑dollar purchase commitments to accelerate U.S. capacity and R&D for advanced optics used in AI infrastructure. Management subsequently reported on May 6, 2026 that AI datacenter and communications demand was driving revenue acceleration and margin expansion, supported by record backlog, orders extending into 2028, and multiple LTAs with customer prepayments and capacity commitments. Q3 FY2026 results highlighted Datacenter & Communications revenue of $1.362B (+40.6% y/y), now 75% of mix, with near‑term growth led by 800G, a rapidly ramping 1.6T transceiver cycle, and growing OCS shipments as internal bottlenecks eased. Coherent raised its OCS TAM view to >$4B, shipped first transceivers using 6‑inch InP, and outlined CPO content breadth with initial H2 CY2026 revenue and broader scale‑up in H2 CY2027. Indium phosphide capacity was set to double by next quarter, with roughly half at 6‑inch by year‑end, while pricing remained healthy and 1.6T ASPs exceeded 800G. Management flagged execution and cycle risks: the need to double 6‑inch InP output with high yields, concentration in AI datacenter customers under LTAs, timing risk on OCS/CPO adoption, and high capital intensity pressuring cash conversion. Gross margin improved on cost reductions, including 6‑inch IP wafers at roughly half the per‑device cost of 3‑inch, with a reiterated >42% long‑term GM target.
