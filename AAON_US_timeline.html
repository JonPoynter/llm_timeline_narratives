Initial AI data center orders and capacity build-out

2024-10-25 → 2025-10-27
AAON began visibly scaling into AI data center thermal solutions with a large October 2024 liquid cooling order under its BASX brand and a November 2024 purchase of a 787,000 square foot Memphis facility to expand production capacity. Early 2025 disclosures showed a record backlog and that most incremental BASX orders were associated with data center liquid cooling. Partnership announcements and regional economic development materials tied BASX equipment to Applied Digital’s Polaris Forge 1 AI factory and referenced support for xAI’s Memphis operations, while third‑party reporting later in 2025 confirmed the first 50MW of that Applied Digital campus entering service. Collectively, these events signaled accelerating demand from AI infrastructure customers and AAON’s strategic capacity positioning to serve it.

FY25 disclosures and FY26 hypergrowth: backlog records, BASX scale, AI‑centric products

2026-03-02 → 2026-06-02
AAON’s FY25 10‑K explicitly identified core end‑customers in data center cooling and described BASX’s 2025 introduction of a proprietary Coolant Distribution Unit (CDU) engineered for high‑density AI/HPC deployments. Entering FY26, AAON reported record Q1 sales and backlog, with large step‑ups in BASX sales and liquid‑cooling revenue, and communicated a 2026 outlook implying roughly $1B of BASX revenue.
