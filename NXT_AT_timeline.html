Foundational cloud interconnects

2018-07-04 → 2021-07-20
NEXTDC established foundational connectivity to hyperscale AI ecosystems via Google Cloud Interconnects, enabling low-latency hybrid and multi-cloud networking for enterprise and government customers. In 2018, S1 Sydney added two Google Cloud Interconnects, including Partner Interconnect, offering sub-1ms latency access to Google Cloud Platform. By mid-2021, M2 Melbourne extended this footprint with additional Google Cloud Interconnect access points, including options for Dedicated and Partner Interconnect delivered over NEXTDC’s Axon platform. These integrations improved customer experience and optionality, laying groundwork for later AI-related compute deployments that rely on robust, high-performance network paths.

OpenAI for Australia MoU anchored at S7

2025-12-04 → 2025-12-05
OpenAI and NEXTDC signed an MoU under the OpenAI for Australia program to plan and operate a next-generation hyperscale AI campus and large-scale GPU supercluster at NEXTDC’s S7 in Eastern Creek. OpenAI indicated it intends to be an initial offtaker with the option to scale. Australian federal and NSW governments welcomed the proposal, describing an approximately $7 billion data centre project incorporating renewable PPAs and advanced cooling. Sector coverage noted the S7 site could scale up to ~550MW at full build-out.

FY26 H1 build-out, guidance and risk posture

2026-05-22 → 2026-05-22
NEXTDC reported a step-change in demand with record contracted growth, supported by a ~297MW forward order book to be billed through FY29. Development approvals and upgrades progressed across major campuses (S4, M3, M4), with 33MW built in H1 and work advancing across Australia and Asia. FY26 guidance reaffirmed net revenue of A$390–400m and EBITDA of A$230–240m, while capex guidance was lifted to A$2.4–2.7bn. Liquidity stood at A$4.2bn (A$278m cash; A$3.94bn undrawn), and capital formation options included subordinated notes and JVCo partnerships.
