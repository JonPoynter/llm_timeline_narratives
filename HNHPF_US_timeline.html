AI rack scale-up, CPO/1.6T ramps, and capital model shifts

2026-03-16 → 2026-05-14
Foxconn’s 2025 mix shifted decisively toward Cloud & Networking (AI servers), surpassing Smart Consumer Electronics. Management outlined a 2026 plan to at least double AI rack shipments, expand capacity to roughly 2,000 racks/week, and initiate liquid-cooled ASIC server projects—alongside deepening participation in high-value subsystems (GPU modules, SuperNIC, DPU) and in-house components (CPU boards, busbar, manifolds, cold plates). Capital intensity will rise, with 2026 CAPEX guided to be more than 30% higher than 2025 and a partial shift from buy-sell to consignment to optimize utilization and share capex costs with customers. 1.6T and CPO switches are scheduled to enter mass production in Q3 2026, with initial CPO shipments in the tens of thousands, underpinning expectations for strong Q2 and full-year 2026 growth driven by AI demand. Management flagged cycle, customer concentration, and ramp execution risks.

U.S. AI infra scale-up; OpenAI collaboration and Alphabet robotics JV

2025-08-18 → 2025-11-26
Foxconn monetized and repurposed its Ohio facility, selling it to SoftBank for $375 million while continuing to operate it as a site to produce AI servers and components for Stargate-related data center buildouts, indicating integration with next-generation AI infrastructure initiatives. Concurrently, Foxconn secured Wisconsin regulatory approval for an additional $549 million investment to expand U.S. operations for AI servers. On partnerships, Foxconn entered a collaboration with OpenAI focused on U.S. manufacturing readiness and co-design of AI infrastructure hardware—with OpenAI providing insight into emerging needs and retaining an option to purchase. Foxconn also launched a joint venture with Alphabet’s Intrinsic to deploy AI-driven robotics in its U.S. factories, leveraging Intrinsic Flowstate and Alphabet’s applied research expertise.
