Ecosystem build-out and AI grid/data center partnerships

2025-03-25 → 2025-11-19
Schneider deepened its role in AI-era infrastructure through collaborative R&D and large-scale customer commitments. It joined EPRI’s DCFlex initiative with leading technology companies to demonstrate how data centers can better support and stabilize the electric grid. Commercially, Schneider and AVAIO Digital pre-procured critical power and cooling equipment for four new AI-ready US campuses. On the software side, Schneider debuted its AI-enabled One Digital Grid Platform running on Microsoft Azure and integrating Azure OpenAI service for utility grid modernization. The company also expanded a long-standing partnership with Switch via a two-phase $1.9 billion supply capacity agreement for power and cooling systems aimed at AI factories, underscoring substantial demand visibility.

Foundation of GenAI integrations and sustainability alliances

2023-11-13 → 2024-03-11
Schneider Electric began formalizing its generative AI approach by integrating Microsoft Azure OpenAI to power customer- and employee-facing copilots, including Resource Advisor Copilot and the internal Jo-Chat GPT assistant. Microsoft’s customer story subsequently highlighted the firm’s broader use of Azure OpenAI Service across the organization. In parallel, Schneider expanded ecosystem ties with leading technology companies through the Catalyze program, adding Google, ASM, and HP as sponsors alongside Intel and Applied Materials to accelerate renewable energy usage across the semiconductor supply chain. These steps established both a practical AI application footprint and stronger collaboration channels with hyperscalers and semiconductor ecosystem leaders.
