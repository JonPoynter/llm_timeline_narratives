North America data center expansion and tenant mix established

2017-10-26 → 2021-06-22
From late-2017 through 2021, Mapletree Industrial Trust (MIT) expanded into North American data centers via joint ventures and portfolio acquisitions, establishing a meaningful footprint tied to enterprise and colocation demand. In October 2017, MIT and Mapletree Investments launched the Mapletree Redwood Data Centre Trust to acquire 14 U.S. data centers for approximately S$1bn ($750m), providing initial scale and diversification across tenants and markets. In September 2019, MIT and Mapletree Investments formed a 50:50 JV to acquire 10 Powered Base Building data centers and co-invest in three Turn-Key Flex hyperscale data centers from Digital Realty, for a combined consideration of about US$1.37 billion. The assets were fully leased with long WALEs and predominantly triple-net structures, reinforcing cash flow visibility. Subsequent disclosures in 2020–2021 detail tenant concentration and composition, with top tenants including AT&T and Equinix and an anonymized global social media company, indicating exposure to cloud and connectivity ecosystems without naming specific frontier AI model providers.

Portfolio optimization, financing actions, and leasing/backfill amid North America headwinds

2026-04-29 → 2026-06-27
By April 2026, MIT reported AUM of S$8.3bn with data centers comprising 57.3% of AUM and a North America WALE of 6.3 years. Leasing momentum included approximately 400k sq ft executed across North America in FY25/26, with a U.S. backfill noted as close to signing, even as confirmed non-renewals in the North America data center portfolio weighed on FY25/26 results and were expected to impact FY26/27. Management outlined selective North America divestments of S$500m–S$600m to enhance flexibility, reduce debt, and recycle capital over 1–2 years. Financing actions included issuing S$300m 3.25% perpetual securities in March 2026 ahead of redeeming existing perps in May 2026. About S$600m of interest-rate swaps mature in FY26/27, with average borrowing cost potentially rising to ~3.4%–3.5%. Roughly 60% of income is FX-hedged around 1.26 USD/SGD for the next 12 months. Utilities costs are largely pass-through, with a 50% tariff increase estimated to impact DPU by less than 1%. A power study for the Hawthorne asset indicated potential capacity up to 99MW, which could enhance sale appeal but would require significant capex.
