Contracted backlog crystallizes; Q1’26 financing and buildout milestones

2026-02-26 → 2026-06-30
By February 2026, TeraWulf reported 522 contracted critical IT MW totaling over $12.8 billion of revenue and said contracted capacity was fully funded with construction aligned to tenant timelines. Management outlined long‑term targets (250–500 MW/yr), advanced Kentucky and PJM/Maryland regulatory steps, and highlighted execution and refinancing risks. Fluidstack leases, Google credit backstops, and Texas JV

2025-08-14 → 2025-12-18
In August 2025, TeraWulf signed landmark AI hosting agreements with Fluidstack at Lake Mariner, initially exceeding 200 MW over ten years. Google agreed to backstop Fluidstack’s obligations, and received warrants equating to ~8% pro forma equity, reinforcing tenant credit quality and enabling project financing. Days later, Fluidstack exercised expansion rights for an additional 160 MW (CB‑5), lifting Google’s aggregate backstop to ~$3.2 billion and pro forma stake in TeraWulf to ~14%. CBRE cited an initial transaction value of ~$6.7 billion over ten years (with potential to ~$16 billion). In October 2025, TeraWulf and Fluidstack formed a Texas joint venture at Abernathy for 168 MW under a 25‑year lease (~$9.5 billion contracted revenue) with ~$1.3 billion in Google credit support; project‑level financing was successfully priced in December 2025.

Groundwork for AI/HPC pivot and first AI tenant

2024-10-10 → 2025-01-06
TeraWulf began repositioning its Lake Mariner campus for high‑performance computing and AI by extending its ground lease to a potential 80‑year term, expanding the land area to 157 acres, and securing exclusive access to up to 750 MW of infrastructure capacity. This created long‑dated site control and scalability for future AI data center developments. In early 2025, the company signed a ten‑year agreement with Core42 for more than 70 MW at Lake Mariner. The deployment is tailored for AI workloads, including direct liquid‑cooled Dell GPU servers, marking one of WULF’s first material AI/HPC customer wins and validating the site’s shift toward AI infrastructure.
