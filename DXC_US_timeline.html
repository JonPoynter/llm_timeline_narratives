OASIS launch, execution risks, bookings/FCF metrics, and Anthropic global alliance

2026-01-01 → 2026-06-30
In fiscal Q4 2026, DXC formally launched OASIS, its AI-native orchestration platform, within a broader pivot toward AI-enabled Fast Track offerings. Management simultaneously flagged execution and commercialization risks and guided to a gradual ramp focused on customer proof points. Q4 FY26 bookings were $3.3B (book-to-bill 1.07x); FY26 bookings were $12.4B (TTM book-to-bill 0.98x). Free cash flow was $110M in Q4 and $713M for FY26, with FY27 FCF guided to approximately $600M. The company reiterated that it does not disclose RPO/backlog, instead reporting bookings and book-to-bill. On June 11, 2026, DXC and Anthropic announced a multi-year global partnership, elevating DXC to a Global Premier partner in the Claude Partner Network. Anthropic’s Claude is already powering DXC OASIS, and the companies cite more than 50 joint customers alongside plans to train a large cadre of Claude-certified engineers embedded in customer environments.

Scaling applied GenAI: ESA platform, AI Workbench, and DeepSeek cost dynamics

2024-01-01 → 2025-12-31
DXC moved from capability build-out to enterprise deployments in 2025. The European Space Agency awarded DXC a contract to design and build an enterprise AI platform, accelerated by NVIDIA infrastructure and based on generative AI from Mistral AI, branded “Ask ESA.” Separately, DXC introduced its AI Workbench with Ferrovial as an anchor client, deploying over 30 specialized AI agents on Microsoft Azure integrated with core back-office systems. DXC also published a DeepSeek-R1 review emphasizing materially lower inference economics relative to GPT-4o and discussing deployment options, including securely hosted full-stack services with Nvidia compute. This underscores DXC’s advisory stance on model selection and total-cost-of-ownership optimization.

GenAI centers and partner previews across Microsoft and AWS

2023-01-01 → 2023-12-31
In 2023, DXC accelerated its GenAI posture. In July, the company launched a Center of Competence in Spain focused on Microsoft technologies, explicitly highlighting Azure OpenAI and ChatGPT and staffing it with 150 DXC experts in Santiago de Compostela. In December, DXC added an AWS Bedrock collaboration to its GenAI center, expressly calling out model access spanning Anthropic Claude, Meta’s Llama, AI21Labs, Stability.ai, and Cohere. DXC was also selected for the Microsoft Security Copilot Partner Private Preview, working with Microsoft product teams on validation, scenarios, and API extensibility. Collectively, these steps expanded DXC’s multi-cloud, multi-model capabilities and positioned it as an implementation and advisory partner for enterprise GenAI adoption.
