Hyperscaler LTAs, capacity expansion, and regional DC builds

2026-01-01 → 2026-06-27
In Q1 2026, Prysmian emphasized that data‑center demand is a unique opportunity for its Digital Solutions and energy portfolios, highlighting its position as a provider of both digital and energy solutions for data centers. Management disclosed ongoing negotiations for multi‑year hyperscaler/data‑center agreements, including ‘inside’ data‑center connectivity. The company guided that long‑term agreements (LTAs) under negotiation span 3–7 years and may include customer down payments, echoing its Transmission model. To support demand, Prysmian outlined a plan to expand optical cable capacity by roughly 40% with investment north of €1 billion, primarily in the U.S. footprint. North American Industrial & Construction also saw strength linked to data‑center demand, and Digital Solutions delivered higher margins supported by fiber demand for data centers, 5G, and FTTH. Management nonetheless flagged that a slowdown or mix shifts (including pricing) could temper growth. Externally, Reuters reported Prysmian’s exploration of sizable M&A as hyperscaler deals neared, while regionally the company partnered with Ufinet to deploy a 30 km underground network interconnecting hyperscale data centers in Alphaville, Barueri (SP), underscoring ongoing near‑term project activity.

Hollow‑core bets and data‑center positioning

2025-03-21 → 2025-12-31
In 2025 Prysmian moved early to align its fiber portfolio with AI and hyperscale data‑center requirements. In March 2025, the company announced a partnership focused on high‑volume production of hollow‑core optical fiber and cable targeted at operators of data centers required for the AI economy. This sharpened its product roadmap toward ultra‑low latency interconnects. By July 2025, Prysmian complemented this with a strategic investment in Relativity Networks, framing the tie‑up as a long‑term partnership to industrialize hollow‑core fiber technology as a backbone for the data‑center build‑out. These steps established product/technology optionality ahead of an anticipated acceleration in hyperscale demand.
