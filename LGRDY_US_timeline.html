Scale-up, AI/DC mix, and 2026 operational updates

2026-02-02 → 2026-06-23
In 2026, Legrand reported that data centers are central to growth, reaching €2.4bn (26% of 2025 sales) and trending toward around 30% of 2026 sales with potential to reach 35%–40%. Management cited strong U.S. data center performance, capacity expansion at steady capex intensity (~2% of sales), and multi‑year hyperscaler investment supporting demand. The company also flagged an acquisition program reinforcing data centers and energy transition, while noting transparency limits (no RPO/backlog disclosure) and potential concentration/cyclicality risk. Operationally, Legrand extended AI capabilities and platform reach: RackLink Cloud became vendor‑agnostic with AI Teammate features via Xyte, and the firm delivered integrated infrastructure for a new AI/HPC data center at Yerevan State University (64 NVIDIA H100 GPUs) under a €3m budget and accelerated timeline. Euronext disclosures also referenced 2025 launches of agentic tools (Gaia, Elia) and an investment in Accelsius, a direct‑to‑chip liquid cooling pioneer.

AI data center positioning and GenAI internalization

2024-10-22 → 2025-06-05
By late 2024, Legrand emphasized portfolio readiness for AI data center deployments, citing support for NVIDIA DGX SuperPOD environments and a full stack of AI‑ready products across cabinets/containment, power, connectivity, and monitoring. In parallel, Legrand began deploying generative AI for internal and customer‑facing operations. Reported benefits included significant content generation time reductions and user‑experience improvements; Elia’s coverage was expanding toward the full product catalog, indicating growing enterprise GenAI adoption.

Early AI and voice-assistant integrations

2017-05-02 → 2019-01-07
From 2017 through 2019, Legrand established early integrations of its connected home and building solutions with leading voice-assistant ecosystems and began embedding AI-driven features with major platform partners. The company announced Google Assistant compatibility for its On-Q/radiant and adorne lighting systems in 2017, followed by the launch of the 'Works with Legrand' interoperability program in early 2018 to accelerate third‑party integrations via APIs. Across 2018–2019, Legrand publicized gradual incorporation of AI technologies into its solutions and broad compatibility with Amazon Alexa, Google Assistant, Apple Siri, and Microsoft Cortana. It also showcased Microsoft Office 365 workspace optimization using sensors and AI logic for room release, and in early 2019 introduced Alexa ‘built‑in’ functionality for certain wiring devices while maintaining multi‑assistant compatibility.
