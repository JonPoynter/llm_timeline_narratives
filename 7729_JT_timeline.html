FY2026 AI/HPC demand cycle and operational scaling

2026-05-13 → 2026-05-13
Management reported continued strength in High Performance Computing (including generative AI) as a key driver for semiconductor equipment demand. Mix within HPC orders skewed toward Logic in 2H, while AI-package grinder demand and very high utilization at key plants underscored tight supply conditions; lead times extended to around five months for probers and beyond six months for grinders. China is expected to remain roughly 30% or more of total SPE sales, and hybrid bonding mass production is expected to accelerate toward CY2028 with more concrete discussions anticipated from FY2027/3 2H, including contributions to NAND applications. Financially, FY2026 orders rose 12% y/y to ¥163.096bn, while backlog declined 4.5% y/y to ¥79.359bn. Investment levels remained solid with capex around ¥11.1bn and R&D around ¥12.0bn, and free cash flow was approximately ¥13.5bn. The SPE segment posted a 26% operating margin in Jan–Mar on a higher-value mix, with further margin improvement expected as the mix improves. Management highlighted several risks: sensitivity to the AI/HPC cycle, potential Middle East–related logistics disruptions not embedded in guidance, and the possibility that a declining backlog could weaken revenue visibility into FY2027. 1H FY2027/3 orders are expected to be somewhat concentrated in Apr–Jun.
