Early 2026 ramp: AI/HBM momentum, shipments and capacity investment

2026-03-02 → 2026-06-30
In early 2026, Disco reported further AI/HBM-driven strength, record shipment levels, and robust forward shipment forecasts. The company’s materials point to sustained IC application momentum tied to generative AI and steady consumables, with application mix skewed heavily to IC in both dicers and grinders. Operationally, Disco highlighted high factory utilization, redeployment of about 100 engineers to support output, and continued investment plans (capex above JPY 30 billion annually; approx. JPY 33 billion capex in FY2027 plan) to expand R&D and plant capacity. Management also noted customers prioritizing securing equipment volumes over price, with minimal earnings impact from higher sourcing costs. Alongside positive indicators, Disco explicitly flagged the risk that a slowdown in AI data center spend or a pause in HBM capacity additions would weigh on shipments and margins, underscoring concentration risk tied to AI/HBM spending cycles.

FY2025 AI-driven demand and product expansion

2025-07-17 → 2025-12-31
Through mid-to-late 2025, Disco Corp reported accelerating demand tied to generative AI, with record equipment sales on a shipment basis and high overseas mix. Company-issued materials attribute growth to IC applications for generative AI, with steady consumables alongside softer power-semiconductor demand. The firm complemented this demand backdrop with product announcements aimed at advanced packaging and back-end diversification: a large-panel package dicing saw (DFD6080) and three new laser saws addressing applications where blade dicing is challenged. A December technology briefing emphasized 2.5D packaging for AI accelerators with HBM and logic dies, while maintaining strict non-disclosure of specific customer details. Overall, 2H CY2025 marked a period of strong AI-related adoption signals and portfolio broadening to serve fan-out and panel-level packaging trends, setting the stage for further momentum into 2026.
