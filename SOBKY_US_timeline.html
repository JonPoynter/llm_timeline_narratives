Monetization, infrastructure scale-up, and financing sensitivity

2026-01-01 → 2026-06-30
In 2026 SoftBank Corp emphasized that AI monetization would begin in FY2026 and scale to full contribution in FY2027, with Enterprise becoming the key earnings driver. Management highlighted GPU-based operations ramping, Crystal/Crystal intelligence commercialization, sovereign cloud launch sequencing, and a plan to double Cloud & AI revenue within Enterprise. A ~JPY100bn buffer remains to support development-stage areas (including AI and HAPS), while minority interests including SB OAI Japan were noted as drags on net income. Operationally, SoftBank announced a joint validation with Ampere to improve small-model inference on CPUs via Ampere-optimized llama.cpp, and with OpenAI launched a cybersecurity ‘patching service’ targeting Japan’s top 3,000 critical-infrastructure companies. Externally, Group-level developments underscored both scale and financing sensitivity: a €75B/5 GW AI data center plan in France, a Vision Fund gain from OpenAI stake revaluation, Helion’s new funding round (with SVF2 participation), and reports that a $6B OpenAI-backed margin loan had stalled.

OpenAI alignment, JV formation, and global infrastructure ambitions

2025-01-21 → 2025-12-31
In 2025, SoftBank’s ecosystem deepened ties with OpenAI. A global-scale "Stargate" initiative was announced alongside OpenAI and others, signaling a multi-hundred-billion-dollar GPU infrastructure push. SoftBank Group and OpenAI unveiled plans for "Cristal intelligence" and a Japan-focused joint venture (SB OAI Japan) that would be consolidated to SoftBank Corp and exclusively market the service domestically; SoftBank Corp outlined a pay-for-use approach and a potential role providing local data center land, buildings, and electricity while Stargate would supply GPUs. The JV was formally launched in November 2025, with SoftBank Corp slated as the first user ahead of a 2026 market rollout. Meanwhile, SoftBank Group rebalanced its AI portfolio, including selling its Nvidia stake—a move that pressured AI shares but freed capital.

Early pilots and model diversification

2024-02-06 → 2024-12-31
SoftBank Corp initiated concrete generative AI pilots with external providers, starting in early 2024. It collaborated with Miyazaki City to test Google Cloud Vertex AI for public-sector workflows and separately began co-developing and rolling out Azure OpenAI Service to automate its own call center operations, targeting shorter wait times and more consistent responses. These efforts established a multi-model footing across OpenAI (via Microsoft Azure) and Google (Vertex AI), while demonstrating near-term, operations-focused use cases inside SoftBank Corp and with a municipal customer.
