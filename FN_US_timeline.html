AI datacom ramps amid supply constraints; major capacity build‑out

2026-01-01 → 2026-06-27
In the May 2026 quarter, Fabrinet highlighted strong AI/datacom demand but faced component shortages—lasers, memory, and certain ASICs—that constrained shipments and created near‑term revenue recognition risk. Management emphasized the constraint was on supply rather than demand, with multiple 800G hyperscaler scale‑out programs already shipping and expected to ramp to full run‑rate by mid‑FY2027. Additional merchant transceiver programs were slated to begin production in 2H CY2026, alongside ongoing CPO, OCS, DCI, and HPC manufacturing engagements. To support growth, Fabrinet advanced a substantial Thailand capacity expansion program: Building 10, the Navanakorn clean‑room factory acquisition, and Pinehurst conversion. The company detailed a staged Building 10 ramp, significant datapoints on capacity additions (current capacity ~$4.8B, adding ~$3.0B from Building 10, and path toward ~$8.5B with potential to ~$11.5B), and near‑term free cash flow pressure from elevated capex. Management also reiterated customer concentration risk and that supply constraints were likely to persist into the subsequent quarter.
