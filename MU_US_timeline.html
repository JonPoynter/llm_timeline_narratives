Anthropic pact, multi‑year SCAs, record Q3 and raised Q4 outlook

2026-06-22 → 2026-06-26
Micron signed a strategic agreement with Anthropic, including a multiyear memory supply pact, AI architecture collaboration, enterprise adoption of Claude across Micron, and a strategic equity investment. Days later, Micron reported record fiscal Q3 results and introduced Q4 guidance far above consensus. The company disclosed 16 Strategic Customer Agreements (SCAs) with take‑or‑pay features totaling around $100B in minimum contracted revenue and about $22B in deposits/commitments (including ~$18B cash and ~$4B letters of credit). Management detailed imminent RPO disclosure (>$5B for Q3-signed SCAs with ~$1.8B NTM and broader ~$100B expected in Q4), indicating roughly 25% revenue coverage at floor pricing initially and an ultimate target of ~40% under RPO-type commitments. While pricing floors/ceilings support margin durability, Micron also flagged risks: normalization of supply/pricing, ramp execution for HBM4/HBM4E, delivery obligations under SCAs, and record capex potentially pressuring FCF if demand underperforms. Shares surged, with market cap briefly surpassing Meta and near Tesla levels on June 25 after the guidance beat.
