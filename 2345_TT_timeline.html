OCP-era collaborations and open networking foundation

2015-03-10 → 2019-11-18
Accton and its subsidiary Edgecore Networks anchored themselves in the open networking ecosystem through collaborations tied to Facebook/Meta and the Open Compute Project (OCP). In 2015, Accton/Edgecore contributed open data center switch designs to OCP and commercialized Facebook’s Wedge design, reinforcing ties with a major hyperscaler customer and the OCP community. In 2016, Accton-built Wedge 100 switches were accepted by OCP and delivered via Edgecore, further signaling production-grade scale. In 2018–2019, Accton/Edgecore featured in OCP-aligned initiatives spanning NOS innovation (with Facebook and Google on ONL-based approaches) and Meta’s F16/Minipack fabric architecture contributions. Edgecore introduced a commercial Minipack platform aligned to Meta’s OCP design, with additional visibility in SONiC/Cumulus ecosystems. Industry coverage over this period positioned Accton among leading white-box hardware suppliers deployed by large hyperscale clouds.
