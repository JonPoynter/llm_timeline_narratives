Foundational hyperscale load growth and early renewables enablement

2010-08-27 → 2019-12-31
From 2010 through 2019, Dominion Energy Virginia emerged as a key utility partner for hyperscale data centers in the Commonwealth. Microsoft’s 2010 siting decision in Mecklenburg County cited Dominion’s reliable and competitively priced power as instrumental, establishing an early anchor for cloud infrastructure in the state. The period also included a 2015 special rate contract filing for Vadata (an Amazon/AWS affiliate), reflecting tailored tariff structures for large, high load‑factor data center customers. Dominion collaborated on renewable power solutions for hyperscalers, including a 20 MW solar project announced in 2016 with Microsoft and a new renewable rate option (Schedule RF) to serve Facebook’s Richmond‑area data center in 2017. The partnership with Facebook expanded in 2019 to dedicate six additional solar facilities totaling 350 MW. Broader AI ecosystem developments—such as Microsoft’s $1B investment in OpenAI in 2019—foreshadowed intensifying AI workloads that would, in turn, reinforce long‑run power demand for data centers Dominion serves.
