Scale-up financing, capacity expansion, and AI-cycle execution

2026-01-01 → 2026-06-27
Advantest’s FY2025 results, released April 27, 2026, credited record performance to surging AI-related test demand. The company secured funding via ¥100b zero-coupon convertible bonds (due 2031), allocating proceeds toward capacity expansion, strategic inventory, and next-gen testing solutions. Management targeted SoC tester capacity of 7,500 systems in the near term and outlined a further step to 10,000 systems per year by end-CY2028/into 2029. The outlook emphasized AI data center build-outs and inferencing trends, while flagging supply-chain tightness and FY2026 margin headwinds. Guidance pointed to strong FY2026 top-line and profitability with increased R&D and capex. The period also featured external market volatility around AI-linked equities and sector rebounds, plus continued ESG ecosystem participation via SEMI’s SCC.

HBM-led acceleration and profit revision

Through 2024, Advantest’s memory test business was buoyed by rapid adoption of high bandwidth memory (HBM) for AI workloads. By October 2024, the company raised its full-year operating profit outlook as AI-related chip testing demand set new records, with quarter results tripling year-on-year and HBM capacity ramp plans underway.

AI demand ignition and positioning

2023-04-01 → 2023-12-31
In early 2023, Advantest explicitly tied rising orders to the surge in interest around OpenAI’s ChatGPT and broader AI training buildouts. Management highlighted that leading AI chip vendors such as Nvidia and AMD were increasing purchases of test equipment, benefiting Advantest as a dominant supplier. The company also used its VOICE 2023 developer conference to spotlight AI themes and engage ecosystem stakeholders, featuring speakers from major AI and technology organizations and its own Chief AI Officer. Guidance communications in April 2023 further indicated that ChatGPT-linked compute projects were expected to begin impacting sales from late FY2023 into FY2024.
